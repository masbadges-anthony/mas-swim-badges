MAS BADGES — Assessment System Handbook

**Malaysia Aquatics · Akuatik Malaysia** *How BADGES levels are
assessed, recorded, certified, and audited — the operating procedure for
assessment*

**Version 1.0** **Custodian:** Chief Examiner A companion to the BADGES
Manual and the 7-Level Syllabus · Structured on the SwimSafer CAMS
Handbook format, adapted to MAS Badges policy

About this Handbook

The BADGES Program is Malaysia Aquatics' national Learn-to-Swim
certification framework. A swimmer progresses through seven levels —
Starfish, Sea Turtle, Guppy, Octopus, Frog, Swordfish, Dolphin — each
awarded after the swimmer demonstrates the published skills for that
level to a MAS Certified Examiner.

This Handbook is the **operating procedure** for those assessments. Its
place in the document system:

- It **assesses to the standard** set in the **7-Level Syllabus** — and
  never restates the criteria. The Syllabus is the single source;
  examiners assess strictly against its right-hand column.

- It is required by the **BADGES Manual**, which sets policy (*that*
  assessment must be credible, independent, auditable, anonymized). The
  Manual governs where the two ever appear to differ.

- Only persons certified through the **Examiner Certification Course
  Guide** may assess.

- Everything runs through the **BADGES Platform Portal**; the Portal
  enforces, this Handbook describes the human procedure.

**Custody and change route (Decision R6 — adopted from v1.0).** The
Chief Examiner holds and maintains this Handbook. *Operational* details
may be revised by the Chief Examiner with the Chairperson's endorsement,
**without re-ratifying the BADGES Manual** — this is what keeps the
Manual thin and the Handbook agile. Changes that touch the *standard*
(criteria, level definitions) are not made here; they follow the
Syllabus change route (Coaching Panel approves → Board ratifies).

> ***How MAS Badges differs from a centralised system.** MAS does not
> run fixed assessment centres on a fixed calendar. Assessments are
> requested by an instructor or centre, scheduled by MAS, and conducted
> by a state examiner at an approved venue — on demand, wherever
> candidates are ready. This Handbook adopts the discipline of a
> centralised system (controlled assessment day, integrity safeguards,
> structured re-attempts, central release of results) while keeping the
> distributed, booking-driven model that suits Malaysia.*

**The standing principle.** *An award shall never be lowered to permit a
candidate to gain a success for which they are not properly prepared.*

1\. Overview

The seven levels form a continuous pathway from a child's first day in
the water to competitive readiness. Assessment is by **practical only**
(see §4). Each level is awarded on a recorded **Pass**; an unmet
criterion yields a **Refer**. There is no partial pass and no skipping
levels.

2\. General

2.1 The BADGES Platform Portal

The system runs on two linked addresses: the public website
**www.masbadges.org** (programme information and certificate
verification) and the management portal **apps.masbadges.org**, the
BADGES Portal. The Portal supports the administration of assessments and
is accessible to stakeholders in their respective roles — instructors,
centres, examiners, and MAS governance.

2.2 Registration on the Portal

- It is the responsibility of every instructor and centre to ensure that
  they, and their candidates, are registered on the Portal.

- A candidate is registered by their instructor or centre (**not**
  directly by a parent). A candidate not registered on the Portal cannot
  be registered for, or take part in, any assessment.

2.3 The assessment session lifecycle (Portal mapping)

Assessment is session-centric. A booking opens a session; an examiner is
invited and must accept before scheduling; outcomes are recorded against
the session; certificates issue from it.

| **State**                             | **What happens**                                                                                                                           |
|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Requested**                         | Instructor/centre submits the roster, venue, and tentative date. Pro-forma bill generated.                                                 |
| **Examiner invited**                  | Staff invite COI-eligible examiners (the COI trigger is the hard backstop).                                                                |
| **Scheduled**                         | An examiner accepts → becomes the session examiner; roster assigned to them.                                                               |
| **Completed**                         | Examiner records Pass/Refer per candidate per level.                                                                                       |
| **Invoiced → Paid**                   | Invoice settled → certificates bulk-issued and verifiable → receipt issued.                                                                |
| **Examiner paid → Closed → Archived** | Examiner payout recorded with proof → session closes when both legs settle → archived. *(Confirmed, R2: MAS is the payment intermediary.)* |

The examiner sees only assessment essentials (candidate name and level).
Billing, claim status, and family details are not shown to the examiner.
The Portal is authoritative for the operational state of any session.

3\. Practical Assessments

3.1 Assessment venues & scheduling

No fixed centres, no fixed hours. An assessment is held at a
MAS-approved venue (§6) on a date arranged for that booking. MAS
schedules the session and assigns a state examiner, deployable across
state lines where a state has no available examiner (Manual §9).
Sessions run on rolling demand, subject to examiner availability and
venue approval; the requesting party proposes date and venue, MAS
confirms both and assigns the examiner.

3.2 Booking, rescheduling, cancellation, absentees, weather

**3.2.1 Booking.** All bookings are made on the Portal by the
candidate's instructor or centre, at least **30 days** before the date.
Where clubs combine, the lead instructor is coordinator (§5). Every
candidate registration must be validated by the recognised MAS
instructor tagged to that candidate, confirming readiness for the target
level.

**3.2.2 Rescheduling.** Permitted up to the booking deadline (30 days
before the date). After that, where the session has met the conditions
to proceed, rescheduling is not permitted except for the
inclement-weather and valid-medical cases below.

**3.2.3 Cancellations.** Once payment is made and the session confirmed,
cancellations are not permitted. A party unable to attend should
reschedule before the deadline. Confirm availability before paying.

**3.2.4 Absentees.** A no-show forfeits that session's slot; no refund
for absence without a valid reason. A candidate absent for a valid
medical reason may be re-booked on submission of a medical certificate
for MAS verification.

**3.2.5 Inclement weather.** At first thunder or lightning the water is
cleared immediately and the venue's lightning protocol is followed.
After stand-down, the examiner judges — against effective pool time
remaining — whether the session can finish or is a rain-off (Annex A). A
rain-off is not marked; affected candidates re-book at no additional
fee. A candidate already marked absent before the stoppage stays absent.

3.3 Minimum & maximum participants

**3.3.1 Minimum. No hard floor** — a session may run for a single
candidate, so small or rural cohorts are never excluded. **Recommended
minimum: five (5)** for an economical scheduled session; below five, a
session runs only at the requesting party's election, bearing the full
fee and any examiner travel allowance.

**3.3.2 Maximum per level & session.** Caps protect observation quality
and safety; the per-level maximum (one examiner) falls as the level
rises.

| **Level** | **Badge**  | **Max / session** | **Water**      |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1         | Starfish   | 16                | Shallow        |
| 2         | Sea Turtle | 14                | Shallow        |
| 3         | Guppy      | 12                | Shallow / deep |
| 4         | Octopus    | 10                | Shallow / deep |
| 5         | Frog       | 8                 | Shallow / deep |
| 6         | Swordfish  | 8                 | Shallow / deep |
| 7         | Dolphin    | 6                 | Deep only      |

- Up to three (3) adjacent levels may be assessed in one session; the
  total is governed by the staffing table (Annex B) and examiner time,
  whichever is more restrictive.

- A single examiner assesses up to **30** candidates per session; above
  30 a second examiner runs a parallel group, capped at **60** total
  (Annex B).

3.4 On assessment day

Three roles run a safe assessment and are kept separate — the examiner
assesses only; a Water-Safety Supervisor watches the water; an Assistant
Facilitator marshals (Annex B). The day:

- Examiner arrives ≥ 15 minutes early, inspects the pool, confirms the
  water-safety team is present, sets up registration.

- **Registration & consent gate.** Each candidate is checked against the
  roster. A candidate without completed parental consent and the
  Safe-to-Swim Declaration / Release of Liability (F-SHR-01) on file
  (§3.10) does not take part. Walk-ins not on the roster are not
  assessed.

- **Safety briefing** opens every session; a candidate arriving after it
  has started may not join that session.

- **Holding areas.** Candidates in the candidate area; parents/coaches
  separate, with no contact with the examiner during assessment.

- **One group in the water at a time, per examiner** — candidates enter
  only when called.

3.5 Re-attempts

Where a candidate cannot complete a criterion on the first try, the
examiner may permit up to **three (3) re-attempts for that criterion**,
at discretion, giving specific instruction before each. Re-attempts are
not guaranteed; the examiner weighs time available, group size and
ability, other criteria already missed, weather impact, and the
candidate's safety and condition. Failing a criterion after the
permitted re-attempts is a Refer for that level. *(A referred level may
be re-attempted at any future session — no penalty, no limit; see
§3.7.)*

3.6 Payments

Assessment fees follow the level and are **paid to MAS in advance** by
the requesting party — never collected at the poolside. The schedule
below is seeded from Manual §16; the **authoritative source of fee
quanta is the Portal fee schedule** (Decision R3).

| **Levels** | **Badge**                         | **Fee (per candidate)** |
|------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------|
| 1–3        | Starfish, Sea Turtle, Guppy       | RM 50                   |
| 4–7        | Octopus, Frog, Swordfish, Dolphin | RM 75                   |

The Portal fee schedule is authoritative; the current per-level amounts
are recorded in Manual Appendix F.

- For a combined session, payment is made in one coordinated payment
  through the lead instructor, in advance.

- **Variable-level reconciliation (Decision R2).** Advance payment
  covers the **booked** levels. Where a candidate clears **additional**
  levels on the day (e.g. booked L4, also clears L5), the extra levels
  are **invoiced post-session** before those certificates issue. The
  Portal generates a pro-forma at booking and reconciles the final bill
  after grading.

> *▶ **Decision R2 — billing model (CONFIRMED).** v1.0's policy (payment
> routed through MAS, per level) is retained **and extended**: MAS acts
> as the **payment intermediary** for examiner fees. Each session
> carries two legs — money **in** from the requesting instructor and
> money **out** to the examiner — and does not **close** until both
> settle, with the examiner payout recorded against proof. This
> **revises Manual §14.5** (previously: requesting party pays the
> examiner directly), and the cross-state examiner allowance (Manual
> §9.4) likewise routes through MAS for auditability.*

3.7 Results

- **Pass or Refer — no partial pass.** Pass: every criterion at the
  level is met; badge and certificate awarded. Refer: one or more
  criteria not yet met; the examiner records exactly which, and the
  candidate may re-attempt the level at any future session — no penalty,
  no limit.

- **No results at the poolside.** Outcomes are never read out,
  displayed, or discussed at the pool. They are released centrally on
  the Portal after the examiner submits the record — protecting examiner
  independence and a child's privacy. Results include the breakdown of
  any criteria not met.

3.8 Appeals

A Pass/Refer outcome is the examiner's on-the-water judgement. Because
no result is announced at the pool and no third party witnesses the
assessment, **MAS does not operate a result-appeal process** — there
would be nothing to re-adjudicate. The recourse for a Refer is built
into the pathway: the instructor targets the named gap and the candidate
re-attempts at any future session.

A concern about **how** an assessment was conducted — not the result —
is a Code of Conduct matter (e.g. an undeclared conflict of interest)
and is referred in writing to the Chief Examiner (§7).

> *▶ **Decision R1 — appeals (CONFIRMED — no appeal for now).** This
> position is retained from v1.0 and **supersedes Program Manual
> §11.5**, which currently describes a result-dispute / re-assessment
> process. **Manual §11.5 is to be amended** to (a) remove result
> re-adjudication and (b) reference the re-attempt recourse plus the
> conduct-complaint route. A formal evidence-based appeals pathway would
> be introduced only if MAS later adopts a second-assessor or
> recorded-assessment structure, and would then run as a Portal
> process.*

3.9 Certificates

- MAS issues the certificate and supplies the badge for every Pass.
  Because there is no theory component (§4), a certificate is available
  once the practical assessment is recorded **and the applicable fee is
  settled** (§3.6).

- Certificates are **immutable** once issued; an error is corrected only
  by **revoke-and-reissue**, which is logged. Serials are generated and
  the level/name are snapshotted at issue.

- Public verification on www.masbadges.org confirms a certificate is
  genuine **without ever revealing a minor's name or identifying
  details** — a child-safety hard rule, enforced in the Portal.

3.10 Medical conditions, fitness to swim & release

- **Required for every candidate.** A Safe-to-Swim Declaration and a
  Release of Liability must be on file, prepared and collected by the
  requesting instructor/centre and verified by the examiner at the
  consent gate. A candidate without them does not take part.

- **Declaring a condition.** Any significant or permanent condition that
  may affect performance of a criterion (e.g. certain eye conditions or
  allergies) is declared on the Declaration and flagged to the examiner
  in advance. The examiner may adapt or decline a specific criterion on
  safety grounds, in consultation with MAS — **without lowering the
  criteria standard**.

- **Temporary conditions.** Handled by rescheduling before the deadline,
  or the absentee route (§3.2.4) with a medical certificate.

This Handbook sets the procedure for declaring and accommodating
conditions; it does not give medical guidance.

4\. Theory Component

BADGES certification is by **practical assessment only**. There is
currently no theory quiz, and none is required for a certificate.

> *The current programme has no on-paper theory component for swimmers;
> assessment is practical only. (The examiner-certification theory
> assessment is separate and lives in the Examiner Certification Course
> Guide.)*

5\. Combined & Large Sessions

**5.1 When this applies.** Several clubs/schools combining into one
session, or a single requester bringing a large group into the upper
staffing bands (Annex B). These sit on top of §3.

**5.2 Multiple swim schools / clubs.** A **lead instructor coordinates**
— creates the session on the Portal, is the sole point of contact with
the examiner, and is responsible for the conduct of all candidates and
helpers from the combined group.

**5.3 Booking.** The lead instructor books on the Portal ≥ 30 days
before the date (earlier for the largest sessions). For 21+ candidates,
a Session Coordinator (an Assistant Facilitator) is named as the single
on-the-day liaison (Annex B).

**5.4 Registration.** The lead instructor ensures all candidates are
registered on the Portal no later than 30 days before the date.

**5.5 Venue & timings.** A MAS-approved venue (§6) suiting the highest
level assessed and meeting the staffing requirement for the total
candidates (Annex B).

**5.6 Session limits.** Per-level caps and the staffing table apply in
full, including the second-examiner threshold above 30 and the maximum
of 60.

**5.7 Payment.** One coordinated payment through the lead instructor, in
advance (§3.6).

**5.8 Results, appeals, certificates & medical.** Refer to §3.7, §3.8,
§3.9, §3.10.

6\. Assessment Venues & Pool Criteria

**6.1 Venue approval.** Every venue is approved before use. An
instructor (or the instructor representing a Partner Centre) submits the
proposed venue on the Portal with facility information and photographs;
the Chief Examiner does due diligence against §6.2, requests more if
needed, then lists the venue as MAS-approved. Partner Centres ensure
their own venue remains fit for assessment. **Where the proposed venue
belongs to the Chief Examiner's own centre, the approval is performed by
another examiner or by the Chairperson** (COI-1 recusal; see §7.1).

**6.2 Pool specifications.** Minimums; the Chief Examiner confirms
suitability case by case.

| **Level** | **Min. clear swimming distance** | **Depth**                           | **Other**                       |
|-----------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| 1         | 10 m clear water                 | Shallow — child can stand (≤ 1.0 m) | Safe entry / exit               |
| 2         | 10 m                             | Shallow (≤ 1.0 m)                   | Wall or ledge to grab           |
| 3         | 12 m + out-of-depth area         | Shallow + ≥ 1.5 m for treading      | —                               |
| 4         | 15 m                             | Shallow + ≥ 1.5 m deep              | Wet deck for sit dive           |
| 5         | 25 m course                      | ≥ 1.8 m deep                        | Wall for flip turns; wet deck   |
| 6         | 50 m (or 25 m with turns)        | ≥ 1.8 m deep                        | Wall for tumble / open turns    |
| 7         | 50 m (or 25 m with turns)        | ≥ 1.8 m deep                        | Blocks or safe deck; pace clock |

*MAS reserves the right to halt any assessment where the venue does not
meet these specifications.*

**6.3 Equipment.** The requesting party / venue ensures necessary
equipment is available; a required-equipment list is provided to the
(lead) instructor on booking confirmation.

**6.4 Payment / results / appeals / certificates / medical.** Per §3.6
and §3.7–§3.10.

7\. Examiners

7.1 Examiner Code of Conduct (the controlled form is F-EXM-01)

Every appointed examiner signs this Code on appointment and re-affirms
it at renewal. It is the signed operating instrument. *(Per the document
architecture, the full operating code now lives here; the BADGES Manual
retains only the requirement that examiners hold and sign a code.)*

> ***The conflict-of-interest rule.** **An examiner may not assess any
> candidate whom they have personally taught, coached, or instructed in
> the relevant skills.** If the candidate's own instructor is the only
> examiner available, a different examiner is arranged — from within the
> centre, from another centre, or by cross-state deployment (Manual §9).
> An examiner may assess candidates from their own school provided they
> are not that candidate's regular instructor or coach. **No gift,
> payment, or benefit** beyond the official MAS fee may be accepted from
> a candidate's family.*

- **Standards discipline.** An award is never lowered to grant an
  unearned success; a shortfall is a Refer with a brief written reason.
  The examiner follows the procedure exactly and judges against the
  published criteria, not preference.

- **Confidentiality & data.** Candidate records are confidential, used
  only for MAS certification purposes, never published or repurposed
  without MAS approval.

- **Safeguarding throughout.** Parental consent verified before any
  child takes part; a child is never isolated one-to-one out of sight; a
  child's result and identity are never disclosed poolside or publicly;
  concerns are escalated immediately to the Designated Safeguarding Lead
  (interim: the Chairperson, SG-1).

- **Recusal (Master Trainer & Chief Examiner).** A Master Trainer or
  Chief Examiner may hold a centre affiliation and have students; the
  two material conflicts are blocked at source — centre recognition
  rests solely with the Chairperson, and no examiner assesses a swimmer
  they instruct. They **recuse** from any decision in which their own
  centre is the subject: the Chief Examiner does not approve their own
  centre's venue (§6.1), nor act on its scheduling or complaints, and
  certification of a Master Trainer's own-centre staff is sighted by the
  Coaching Panel. *(Governance Decisions Register, COI-1.)*

- **Breach.** Referred to the Chief Examiner, who investigates and
  reports to the Chairperson; the Coaching Panel decides sanction;
  removal requires Board ratification. Framework in Annex D.

7.2 Assignment for assessments

- MAS assigns the examiner to a confirmed booking and pre-sets them as
  the assessor for each rostered candidate — which is what makes those
  candidates gradeable by that examiner and no other (COI enforced in
  data).

- The examiner confirms date and venue, acknowledges any COI re-routing,
  checks the rostered count against the staffing table, and requires the
  requesting party to provide the water-safety and marshalling team
  before the session is locked.

7.3 Submission of results

The examiner records each outcome at the time of observation and submits
on the Portal — target within 48 hours, maximum seven (7) working days.
A Pass triggers certificate issuance (subject to fee settlement, §3.6).

7.4 Examiner late / no-show

An examiner late or failing to attend a confirmed assessment submits an
explanation. Failure to provide valid reasons results in disciplinary
action under Annex D, up to debarment of examiner status.

7.5 Standing & continuing certification

An examiner conducts at least one assessment per twelve-month period (or
notifies leave), attends the joint annual meeting, and maintains current
**BADGES Instructor** and lifesaving certification. The full
continuing-certification requirements are in the **Examiner
Certification Course Guide**.

Annex A — Inclement Weather Guidance

Guidance only — the examiner's safety judgement always prevails, and the
pool is cleared at first lightning or thunder. "Rain-off" means the
session is not marked and candidates re-book at no additional fee.
Figures are maximum participants per level reasonably completed in the
effective time remaining.

**Lightning before the assessment**

| **Scenario (alert lifted…)** | **Eff. time** | **L1** | **L2** | **L3** | **L4** | **L5** | **L6** | **L7** |
|------------------------------|---------------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 15 min after start time      | ~60 min       | ≤12    | ≤10    | ≤8     | ≤6     | ≤5     | ≤4     | ≤4     |
| 30 min after start time      | ~45 min       | ≤8     | ≤6     | ≤6     | ≤4     | off    | off    | off    |
| beyond 30 min after start    | \<45 min      | off    | off    | off    | off    | off    | off    | off    |

*"off" = rain-off for that level. Lower levels can sometimes continue
when higher cannot, taking less pool time per candidate.*

**Lightning during the assessment**

| **Scenario**                                                                | **Outcome**                                                                      |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Triggered within first 30 min and clears with ≥ ~45 min effective time left | Continue within the "~45 min" caps (L1 ≤8, L2 ≤6, L3 ≤6, L4 ≤4; L5–L7 rain-off). |
| Triggered beyond 30 min in, or insufficient time remains                    | Rain-off — all levels; re-book at no additional fee.                             |

Annex B — Session Limits & Staffing

Two controls replace a fixed permutation grid: per-level caps, and a
staffing team sized to total candidates.

**The three roles.** **Examiner** — assesses only; never the safety
supervisor or marshal. **Water-Safety Supervisor (WSS)** — dedicated to
watching the water for the whole session; holds a current lifesaving
qualification (Bronze Medallion, ILS, or higher) or is a MAS Certified
Instructor; always ≥ 1; never doubles as examiner or marshal.
**Assistant Facilitator** — provided by the requesting party to marshal
candidates, run registration/holding areas, and manage parents; for 21+,
one is named Session Coordinator.

| **Candidates** | **Examiners** | **Water-Safety Supervisors** | **Assistant Facilitators** | **Min. team** |
|----------------|---------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|---------------|
| 1–12           | 1             | 1                            | —                          | 2             |
| 13–20          | 1             | 1                            | 1                          | 3             |
| 21–30          | 1             | 2                            | 1\*                        | 4             |
| 31–45          | 2             | 2                            | 2\*                        | 6             |
| 46–60          | 2             | 3                            | 2\*                        | 7             |

*\\* For 21+, one Assistant Facilitator is the named Session
Coordinator. Deep water (any L4–7 in deep water): ≥ 2 WSS regardless of
band, or one stationed exclusively at the deep end. High-level-heavy
sessions (mostly L6–7) take longer per candidate — treat as one band
smaller. Above 60: split sessions, or apply to the Chief Examiner for a
special multi-examiner arrangement.\*

Annex C — General Reminders for Assessment

**Pre-assessment.** Booking confirmed (≥ 30 days prior); examiner
assigned; date/approved venue acknowledged. COI cases identified and
re-routed. Parental consent + Safe-to-Swim Declaration / Release of
Liability collected for every candidate. Water-safety supervisors and
facilitators arranged per Annex B; Session Coordinator named for 21+.
Fee paid in advance; equipment confirmed; weather checked.

**On the day. Examiner arrives 15 min early; inspects pool; confirms
water-safety team. Registration: roster + identity check; consent /
Safe-to-Swim gate. Candidates to holding area; parents separate; no
parent/coach contact during assessment. Safety briefing delivered; late
arrivals excluded; assess level by level; manage re-attempts. Record
each outcome — do not announce results; thank and dismiss.**

**Post-assessment.** Submit records on the Portal (target 48 h; max 7
working days). MAS releases results and issues certificates/badges for
every Pass. Notify MAS the same day of any rain-off or incident.

Annex E — Frequently Asked Questions

- **Can my child be assessed by their own swim teacher?** No. An
  examiner may not assess a candidate they have personally taught; a
  different examiner is arranged. This is the programme's core integrity
  rule.

- **Can a candidate skip a level?** No. The seven levels are a
  continuous pathway; each is assessed and certified in order.

- **Who books the assessment?** The candidate's instructor or centre, on
  the Portal, ≥ 30 days before the date. Where clubs combine, the lead
  instructor books and coordinates for all.

- **What does "Refer" mean?** One or more criteria not yet met. The
  examiner notes which; the candidate re-attempts at any future session
  — no penalty, no limit.

- **Will I get the result on the day?** No. Results are never announced
  at the pool; MAS releases them on the Portal, with the breakdown of
  any criteria not met.

- **Is there a theory quiz?** No — practical only.

- **What forms must be ready?** Parental consent plus a Safe-to-Swim
  Declaration and Release of Liability for every candidate — prepared by
  the requesting instructor, verified by the examiner before the
  session.

- **What if it rains or there is lightning?** The pool is cleared
  immediately; the examiner decides, on time remaining, whether to
  continue or call a rain-off (re-booked at no additional fee, Annex A).

- **Is there a minimum number of children?** No hard floor — a session
  can run for one candidate. Five is the recommended economical minimum;
  below that the requesting party bears the full cost.

- **How is the venue approved?** An instructor submits the venue with
  photos and details on the Portal; the Chief Examiner checks it against
  the pool criteria and lists it (§6).

- **Can I appeal a result?** Not at present — the recourse for a Refer
  is to re-prepare and re-attempt. A concern about how the assessment
  was conducted (e.g. a conflict of interest) goes to the Chief
  Examiner.

- **How many staff does a session need?** At least the examiner plus one
  Water-Safety Supervisor for up to 12; larger sessions add supervisors,
  facilitators, a Session Coordinator (21+), and a second examiner above
  30, to a maximum of 60 (Annex B).

Annex F — Transitional Swimmers (Previous-Syllabus Holders)

This annex sets out the procedure for a swimmer who holds a recognised
Learn-to-Swim award under a previous syllabus and is entering the BADGES
system. It records the settled procedure only; the living questions

**Settled rule (CD-2).** A swimmer holding a recognised
previous-syllabus award may enter BADGES at the level equivalent to
their prior attainment rather than restarting at Starfish (Level 1). The
award recognises prior attainment; it does not waive assessment — the
swimmer is assessed against the current BADGES criteria for the entry
level. On a pass, a certificate is issued at that level, and the swimmer
pays only for the highest level awarded, not for each intervening level.

**Procedure.**

1\. At registration, the instructor or centre records the prior award
and the level claimed.

2\. The swimmer is entered at the equivalent BADGES level for
assessment.

3\. Assessment proceeds normally against the 7-Level Syllabus criteria
for that level; the session limits in Annex B apply as usual.

4\. On a pass, the Portal issues the certificate at that level;
intervening levels are not separately certified.

5\. The booking is charged for the highest level awarded only, per the
published fee schedule (Manual Appendix F).

**Pending decisions.** Three questions on this provision remain with the
Coaching Panel and are tracked in Manual Appendix D (CD-2): whether
level equivalence is determined automatically or by instructor
judgement; whether a time limit applies to transitional entry; and
whether the Portal enforces the rule or governance handles it manually.
Until these are settled, equivalence is set by the instructor at
registration and corrected by governance where needed.
