# MAS BADGES — Instructor Foundation Certification Course Guide

**Malaysia Aquatics · Akuatik Malaysia**
*The outcomes, criteria, and pathway to certify a BADGES Instructor*

**Edition 1.0** · Companion to the BADGES Manual, the 7-Level Syllabus, and the Teaching System Handbook
**Course delivered by:** the BADGES System Master Trainer (Instructor Course)

---

## About this Guide

This Guide specifies what the **BADGES Instructor Foundation Certification** consists of, who may enter it, and what a candidate must achieve to be recognized as a **MAS Certified Instructor** for the BADGES system. It trains instructors to the doctrine of the **Teaching System Handbook** and the standard of the **7-Level Syllabus**, and it is required by the **BADGES Manual**, which states only *that* instruction must be delivered by certified instructors.

A certified instructor must be able to do three things to standard: teach the seven levels using the IRD method to bring a swimmer genuinely to the Syllabus standard; deliver lessons safely and uphold safeguarding; and operate the **BADGES Platform Portal** to register candidates, issue claim slips, confirm readiness, and book assessments.

**Custody.** This Guide is delivered by the Master Trainer; the underlying teaching doctrine is owned by the Coaching & Technical Board (the Teaching System Handbook). Revisions follow the Coaching Panel route.

> - **(c) Recognize** — holders of a recognized prior MAS Instructor certification are admitted by equivalence, with a bridging module.

## 1. Outcomes — what a certified instructor can do

On certification, a BADGES Instructor can:

- Teach all seven levels using the Introduce → Reinforce → Develop method, building genuine, transferable technique to the Syllabus standard.
- Plan and run safe, well-managed lessons with appropriate supervision and ratios.
- Uphold water safety and safeguarding throughout delivery.
- Judge readiness honestly and prepare a swimmer for independent assessment without teaching to the test.
- Operate the BADGES Portal: register candidates, issue claim slips, confirm readiness, and book assessments — while staying firewalled from judging their own swimmers.

---

## 2. Eligibility and prerequisites

*(Final form subject to Decision OD-1.)* A candidate should:

- Meet the **swimming-competency baseline** for an instructor — able to demonstrate the four strokes and the survival/skill elements across the levels to a competent standard. *(Exact demonstration standard to be confirmed by the Coaching Panel.)*
- Hold (or obtain through this course, per OD-1) the relevant **MAS Instructor certification**.
- Hold a valid **First Aid and CPR**, **Bronze Medallion**, or equivalent lifesaving certification — the same lifesaving baseline an instructor must maintain to act as a Water-Safety Supervisor on assessment day.
- Be in **Good Standing** — no current MAS disciplinary action and no disqualifying criminal record; signs a Good Standing declaration and any required child-protection screening.

> **Founding phase.** During inaugural rollout, MAS recruits from instructors who completed the MAS Instructor Courses in recent intakes and from active swim-school teaching staff, admitting them through the founding-cohort route consistent with the pilot recruitment plan.

---

## 3. Application pathway

1. Submit the **Instructor Application** in the Portal with proof of the swimming-competency baseline, lifesaving certification, the Good Standing declaration, and any child-protection screening.
2. Pay the course registration fee (quantum held in the Portal fee schedule).
3. Attend the full **Instructor Foundation Course** (§5).
4. Meet the **pass requirements** (§6).
5. On certification, the Master Trainer marks the candidate certified, which triggers Portal onboarding (§8).

---

## 4. The Master Trainer (Instructor Course)

The Master Trainer develops and delivers the Instructor Foundation Course, operating in cycles tied to intakes. Responsibilities mirror the examiner side: develop course methodology and materials with the Coaching Panel's approval; plan and deliver each intake, classroom and poolside; lead the practical teaching assessment and recommend certification; submit a course report after each intake; refresh materials in line with Teaching System Handbook and Syllabus changes.

---

## 5. The course — format and modules

**Format.** A multi-day course combining classroom and poolside delivery, with class size capped to allow individual practical teaching assessment of every participant. Attendance at every module is **compulsory**; a missed module means repeating the full course. No partial completions.

| Mod. | Title | Mode | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Program & Governance | Classroom | MAS structure and the BADGES document system; the 7-level pathway; where the instructor fits; the instructor/examiner firewall. |
| 2 | The 7-Level Syllabus, taught | Classroom + Pool | Walk-through of every level's outcomes from a *teaching* lens; what "ready" looks like per level. |
| 3 | The IRD Teaching Method | Classroom + Pool | Introduce → Reinforce → Develop; building technique, not teaching to the test; common faults and correction cues. |
| 4 | Lesson Delivery & Class Management | Classroom + Pool | Lesson structure; supervision; ratios; language and demonstration; managing mixed-ability groups safely. |
| 5 | Water Safety & Safeguarding | Classroom | Water safety integral to every level; child protection and safeguarding obligations; consent; escalation. |
| 6 | Conduct & Professionalism | Classroom | Honest preparation and readiness; respecting examiner independence; transparency with families; use of MAS marks. |
| 7 | **BADGES Portal for Instructors** | Hands-on | **NEW.** Registering candidates; issuing the claim slip; confirming readiness; booking an assessment; what the instructor can and cannot see; the firewall in practice. |
| 8 | Practical Teaching Assessment | Pool | Each candidate delivers a real teaching segment under observation by the Master Trainer. Direct feedback; final certification recommendation. |

---

## 6. Assessment and pass requirements

To be certified as a BADGES Instructor, a candidate must:

- **Attend every module in full.**
- **Pass the theory assessment** covering the Syllabus (taught lens), the IRD method, safety, and conduct. *(Pass mark to be confirmed by the Coaching Panel; recommend aligning with the examiner course's 80%.)*
- **Demonstrate Portal competency** in Module 7 — register a candidate, issue a claim slip, and create a booking correctly.
- **Achieve Competent or above** on the practical teaching assessment in Module 8, judged by the Master Trainer.
- **Sign the relevant conduct undertaking** and complete child-protection screening.

---

## 7. Certification, term, and continuing certification

- **Certification** is recorded by the Master Trainer on completion and triggers Portal onboarding (§8).
- **Continuing certification** requires maintaining current lifesaving and First Aid/CPR certification, upholding safeguarding standards, and completing any refresher modules issued after Teaching System Handbook or Syllabus updates. *(Renewal cycle to be confirmed — recommend aligning with the MAS two-year governance term.)*
- A BADGES Instructor certification is the prerequisite that the **Examiner Certification Course Guide** builds on (subject to OD-1).

---

## 8. Onboarding into the Portal

Certification is the front door to the system, implemented as the instructor-invitation loop:

1. On certification, the candidate's record moves to **certified** in the Portal.
2. An **invitation** keyed to the instructor's email is issued.
3. The instructor **self-signs-up**; the signup is matched to the invitation, and the **instructor role is granted**.
4. The instructor can now register candidates, issue claim slips, and book assessments.

The course and this onboarding are two halves of one pathway: the Course Guide certifies the person; the Portal admits them.

---

## 9. On the BADGES Platform Portal

Instructor competency on the Portal is a certified outcome (Module 7), because the instructor's administrative role is entirely Portal-based: candidate registration (the assessable-eligibility gate), claim-slip issuance to families, readiness confirmation, and assessment booking. The Portal enforces the firewall — a booking instructor is never assigned to assess their own candidates. This Guide certifies the capability; the Portal is where it is exercised.
