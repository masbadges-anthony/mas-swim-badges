# MAS BADGES — 7-Level Swimming Syllabus

**Malaysia Aquatics (MAS) · Akuatik Malaysia**
*The national proficiency standard for the BADGES Learn-to-Swim system — Levels 1 to 7, Starfish to Dolphin*

**Edition 2.0** · Companion to the BADGES Manual
**Custodian:** MAS Coaching & Technical Board

---

## About this Syllabus

This Syllabus is the single authoritative statement of *what a swimmer must be able to do* at each of the seven BADGES levels. It is the **spine of the BADGES document system**: the Manual requires that it exist, the Teaching System Handbook teaches toward it, the Assessment System Handbook assesses against it, the two Course Guides train instructors and examiners to its standard, and the BADGES Platform Portal records every result against it.

It does **not** describe how to teach a skill or how to run an assessment day — those are the Handbooks. It defines only the standard.

### Custody and change control

This Syllabus is owned by the MAS Coaching & Technical Board. Criteria are amended only through the standard syllabus-change route: a change is proposed by the Chief Examiner or Chairperson, **approved by the Coaching Panel, and ratified by the MAS Board**. No party amends the criteria unilaterally. The Chairperson and instructors may *recommend* revisions but do not alter the standard.

### How to read each level

Every level is set out in two columns, and the distinction is deliberate:

- **Teaching Objective** (left) — guidance for instructors preparing candidates, expressed as *Introduce → Reinforce → Develop*. This is the teaching intent, not the pass bar.
- **Assessment Criteria** (right) — the exact, observable tasks an examiner assesses. **Examiners assess strictly against the right-hand column.** A candidate passes a level only by demonstrating every criterion to standard.

Each level also fixes an **Assessment Condition** (water depth and the requirement of independent performance) and the **Badge Awarded** on a pass.

### Assessment principle

All criteria are performed **independently** — without instructor support, flotation aids, or prompting — unless the criterion itself states otherwise. Outcomes are binary at the criterion level and recorded per candidate per level as **Pass** or **Refer**. A refer is not a failure of the swimmer; it identifies the outcome(s) still to be met before the badge is awarded.

### Scope

The BADGES system serves children aged 5–12 as its primary cohort. Progression is **competency-based, not age-based**: a swimmer advances when they consistently meet a level's criteria, at their own pace. The seven levels form one continuous pathway from a child's first day in the water through to competitive readiness.

### Level 7 exit outcome

On completion of Level 7 (Dolphin), a swimmer will be able to:

- Demonstrate sound water safety awareness and self-rescue capability.
- Swim 100 metres each of front crawl, breaststroke, and backstroke, and 50 metres of butterfly.
- Perform all four strokes in accordance with World Aquatics competitive regulations.
- Perform a forward dive from the deck for fly, breast, and free, and an in-water start for backstroke.
- Carry a correct stroke foundation forward into age-group competitive training.

## The pathway at a glance

The seven levels form a continuous developmental pathway; each builds on the one below it.

| Level | Badge | Developmental focus | Stage milestone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Starfish** | Water familiarization, breath control, basic floating and gliding | Comfort and safety |
| **2** | **Sea Turtle** | Self-survival to the wall; basic stroke shapes front and back | Basic mobility |
| **3** | **Guppy** | Front crawl with breathing, backstroke, deep-water treading | Independent swimmer |
| **4** | **Octopus** | Bilateral breathing, breaststroke kick, sit dive, survival backstroke | Three-stroke familiarity |
| **5** | **Frog** | Full breaststroke, dolphin kick, sculling, basic flip turn, squat dive | Stroke breadth |
| **6** | **Swordfish** | Butterfly, breaststroke pullout, tumble and open turns, sidestroke | Competition exposure encouraged |
| **7** | **Dolphin** | All four strokes to competitive distance, IM, backstroke dive, time qualification | Competitive readiness |

**Levels 1–4 — Foundation.** These build the safety and skill foundation. Every Malaysian child who learns to swim should reach at least Level 4, where they have bilateral breathing in front crawl, can dive in safely, and hold fundamental survival skills.

**Levels 5–6 — Stroke development.** These introduce the full four-stroke repertoire and competitive starts and turns. Students at these levels are encouraged to take part in novice meets, swim-splash events, or school-level competitions (MSSD), even before joining a competitive squad. Competition exposure here is part of the developmental design, not an optional extra.

**Level 7 — Competitive bridge.** Level 7 bridges Learn-to-Swim into competitive training, requiring timed front crawl and individual medley alongside all four strokes, turns, and dives at competition standard. A Dolphin badge holder has the technical foundation to enter an age-group competitive program. Partner centres and clubs are encouraged to ensure swimmers reaching Levels 5 and 6 have a clear next step.

## On the BADGES Platform Portal

The Portal is the system of record for this standard. The Syllabus maps onto the Portal as follows:

- **Levels 1–7 are the Portal's level definitions.** This document is the human-readable source for those values; the Portal stores them as the assessed-level enum.
- **Each criterion resolves to a single outcome per candidate per level — `pass` or `refer`** — recorded by the assigned examiner. The Portal does not store partial criteria; the examiner judges the level as a whole against the right-hand column.
- **A certificate is issued only on a recorded `pass`,** gated and serialized, with the level and badge name snapshotted at issue. A `refer` never yields a certificate.
- **Public verification is anonymized:** verifying a Dolphin certificate confirms the level and validity without ever exposing the minor's name or identifying information. This is a hard child-safety requirement of the BADGES system and is enforced in the Portal, not merely stated here.

The Syllabus describes the standard; the Portal records performance against it. Where a level's criteria change through the change-control route above, the Coaching Panel updates this document and the Portal's level definitions in step.
