About this Guide

This Guide specifies what the BADGES Examiner Certification consists of,
who may enter it, and what a candidate must achieve to be recognized as
a **MAS Certified Examiner**. It trains examiners to the doctrine of the
**Assessment System Handbook** and the standard of the **7-Level
Syllabus**, and it is required by the **BADGES Manual**, which states
only *that* examiners must be certified before they assess.

A certified examiner must be able to do three things to standard: judge
a candidate against the Syllabus criteria correctly and consistently;
conduct an assessment safely and impartially per the Assessment System
Handbook; and operate the **BADGES Platform Portal** to accept sessions,
record outcomes, and trigger certification. No one is certified who
cannot do all three.

**Custody.** The Examiner Certification Syllabus is co-owned by the
Chief Examiner and the Coaching Panel; revisions follow the standard
syllabus-change route (Coaching Panel approves → Board ratifies). This
Guide is delivered by the Master Trainer.

1\. Outcomes — what a certified examiner can do

On certification, a BADGES Examiner can:

- Assess any of the seven levels strictly against the Syllabus criteria,
  distinguishing

competent from struggling performance.

- Run an assessment day safely and impartially per the Assessment System
  Handbook —

pre-assessment check, briefing, observation positioning, poolside
calibration, Pass/Refer decisions, and feedback.

- Uphold the Examiner Code of Conduct, with the conflict-of-interest
  rule at the

non-negotiable core.

- Operate the BADGES Portal for the full examiner workflow: accept
  session invitations,

record outcomes, and trigger pass-gated certification.

2\. Eligibility and prerequisites

To be considered for examiner appointment, a candidate must:

- Hold a **current BADGES Instructor certification**. This is a hard
  prerequisite.

- Have a minimum of **two (2) years' active Learn-to-Swim teaching
  experience**.

- Hold a valid **First Aid and CPR**, **Bronze Medallion**, or
  equivalent lifesaving

certification (ILS, React Right, Red Cross, or comparable). Equivalence
is determined by the Chief Examiner with reference to the issuing body.

- Be in **Good Standing** — no current MAS disciplinary action and no
  disqualifying criminal

record; signs the Good Standing & Safeguarding Declaration (F-SHR-03).

- Be familiar with, or willing to learn, the BADGES Portal and the MAS
  process for issuing

certificates and badges.

- Commit to a minimum **two (2) year examiner term**.

3\. Application pathway

1.  Submit the **Examiner Application** in the Portal with proof of a
    current BADGES

Instructor certification, lifesaving certification, teaching record, the
Good Standing declaration, and a brief statement of intent.

2.  Pay the course registration fee (the quantum is held in the Portal
    fee schedule; see §8).

3.  Attend the full **Examiner Certification Course** (2–3 days, §5).

4.  Meet the **pass requirements** (§6).

5.  Receive Coaching Panel ratification on the Chief Examiner's
    recommendation and

Chairperson's endorsement.

6.  Sign the **Examiner Code of Conduct** (F-EXM-01; Assessment System
    Handbook §7.1).

On ratification, the Portal grants the examiner role and generates the
examiner's UID, after which the examiner can be invited to sessions.

4\. The Master Trainer

The **Master Trainer** is the Coaching-Panel-designated authority who
develops and delivers the certification courses. The role is unified — a
single Master Trainer covers both the instructor and examiner streams
(OD-2); this Guide is the examiner stream. The role is functional,
operating in cycles tied to course intakes. Responsibilities for the
examiner stream:

- Co-develop the Examiner Certification Syllabus, methodologies, and
  assessment criteria

with the Chief Examiner; submit to the Coaching Panel for approval and
the Board for ratification.

- Plan, schedule, and deliver each course, classroom and poolside.

- Lead the practical assessment of candidate examiners and recommend
  approvals to the Chief

Examiner.

- Manage procurement of certification kits (certificates and badges) so
  stock is available

for issuance.

- Submit a course report to the Coaching Panel after every intake.

- Maintain course materials and refresh content in line with syllabus
  changes.

full course at the next intake. There are no partial completions.

6\. Assessment and pass requirements

To be approved as a MAS Certified Examiner, a candidate must:

- **Attend every module in full.** Partial attendance is not accepted.

- **Pass the theory assessment** — the BADGES onboarding quiz: **a
  random draw from the examiner question pool** (parameters per Manual
  Appendix F); unlimited retakes; correct answers shown after
  submission. This same quiz gates Portal onboarding.

- **Demonstrate Portal competency** in Module 8 — record a complete mock
  session correctly,

including a Refer with reasons and a triggered certificate issuance.

- **Achieve Competent or above** on the practical assessment in Module
  9, as judged by the

Master Trainer or designated workshop leader.

- **Sign the Examiner Code of Conduct.**

- **Receive Coaching Panel ratification** on the Chief Examiner's
  recommendation and the

Chairperson's endorsement.

7\. Term, renewal, and continuing certification

- **Initial appointment:** two (2) years from approval, aligned with the
  MAS governance term.

- **Continuing certification (good standing)** requires the holder to:
  conduct at least one

assessment per twelve-month period (or notify leave); attend the joint
annual meeting; maintain current **BADGES Instructor certification**;
maintain current First Aid/CPR, Bronze Medallion, or equivalent
lifesaving certification; pass periodic audits by the Chief Examiner;
and complete any refresher modules issued by the Coaching Panel after
syllabus updates.

- **Renewal** requires an active assessment record, satisfactory audits,
  annual-meeting

attendance, and current Instructor and lifesaving certification.

- **Inactivity** (no assessments in 12 consecutive months without
  notified leave) triggers a

standing review. **Audit failure** may lead to supervised assessment
status, retraining at the next course, or removal — at the Coaching
Panel's discretion on the Chief Examiner's recommendation with the
Chairperson's endorsement.

8\. Fees

The course registration fee is set on the single national schedule and
held in the Portal fee schedule (seeded from Manual §16); the current
quantum is recorded in Manual Appendix F.

9\. On the BADGES Platform Portal

The examiner's entire operational life runs through the Portal, which is
why Portal competency is a certified outcome (Module 8), not an
afterthought:

- **Application & UID** — the examiner application is submitted in the
  Portal; on

ratification the examiner role is granted and a UID auto-generated.

- **Session invitations** — the examiner accepts or declines
  invitations; accepting assigns

the roster and makes those candidates gradeable by that examiner and no
other.

- **Recording** — Pass/Refer is recorded per candidate per level; a
  Refer carries reasons;

the COI trigger blocks any candidate the examiner instructed.

- **Certification** — a recorded Pass auto-links to a serialized,
  append-only certificate;

verification is anonymized.

10\. Assessment-day disruption — inclement weather

An assessment session can be interrupted by weather; examiners follow
the Assessment System Handbook §3.2.5 and Annex A on the day. At first
thunder or lightning the water is cleared immediately and the venue’s
lightning protocol is followed before anyone returns to the pool.

If lightning occurs before the session, the start is held; the session
proceeds only if conditions clear within the venue’s stand-down window,
otherwise the affected levels are a rain-off. If it occurs during the
session, whether a level can still be completed depends on the criteria
already assessed and the time remaining — the examiner either finishes
on the evidence already gathered or declares a rain-off for that level.

A rain-off is never recorded as a Refer; affected candidates re-book at
no additional fee. Notify MAS the same day of any rain-off, incident, or
safeguarding concern.
