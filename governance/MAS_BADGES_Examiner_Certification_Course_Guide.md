## About this Guide

This Guide specifies what the BADGES Examiner Certification consists of, who may enter it,
and what a candidate must achieve to be recognized as a **MAS Certified Examiner**. It
trains examiners to the doctrine of the **Assessment System Handbook** and the standard of
the **7-Level Syllabus**, and it is required by the **BADGES Manual**, which states only
*that* examiners must be certified before they assess.

A certified examiner must be able to do three things to standard: judge a candidate against
the Syllabus criteria correctly and consistently; conduct an assessment safely and
impartially per the Assessment System Handbook; and operate the **BADGES Platform Portal**
to accept sessions, record outcomes, and trigger certification. No one is certified who
cannot do all three.

**Custody.** The Examiner Certification Syllabus is co-owned by the Chief Examiner and the
Coaching Panel; revisions follow the standard syllabus-change route (Coaching Panel
approves → Board ratifies). This Guide is delivered by the Master Trainer.

---

## 1. Outcomes — what a certified examiner can do

On certification, a BADGES Examiner can:

- Assess any of the seven levels strictly against the Syllabus criteria, distinguishing
  competent from struggling performance.
- Run an assessment day safely and impartially per the Assessment System Handbook —
  pre-assessment check, briefing, observation positioning, eye-level analysis, Pass/Refer
  decisions, and feedback.
- Uphold the Examiner Code of Conduct, with the conflict-of-interest firewall as the
  non-negotiable core.
- Operate the BADGES Portal for the full examiner workflow: accept session invitations,
  record outcomes, and trigger pass-gated certification.

---

## 2. Eligibility and prerequisites

To be considered for examiner appointment, a candidate must:

- Hold a **current BADGES Instructor certification**. This is a hard prerequisite. *(For the
  founding cohort, see the provision below.)*
- Have a minimum of **two (2) years' active Learn-to-Swim teaching experience**.
- Hold a valid **First Aid and CPR**, **Bronze Medallion**, or equivalent lifesaving
  certification (ILS, React Right, Red Cross, or comparable). Equivalence is determined by
  the Chief Examiner with reference to the issuing body.
- Be in **Good Standing** — no current MAS disciplinary action and no disqualifying criminal
  record; signs a Good Standing declaration.
- Be familiar with, or willing to learn, the BADGES Portal and the MAS process for issuing
  certificates and badges.
- Commit to a minimum **two (2) year examiner term**.

> **Founding cohort provision.** The program has no certified examiners at inaugural
> rollout, so the first cohort is certified through the inaugural course itself; no prior
> examiner experience is required of the founding cohort. A **recognised legacy MAS
> instructor certification is accepted** to enter the founding examiner course in place of
> the BADGES Instructor certification — the course delivers the new rubrics and the poolside
> calibration, and **completing it confers BADGES Instructor standing** (the
> refresher-equivalent), so a founding examiner is directory-listed as a BADGES Instructor
> without a separate instructor refresher (OD-1a). Every other criterion above still
> applies. The inaugural course is co-led by the Master Trainer, the Chief Examiner, the
> Chairperson, and Coaching Panel members.

## 3. Application pathway

1. Submit the **Examiner Application** in the Portal with proof of a current **BADGES
   Instructor certification** (or, for the founding cohort, a recognised legacy instructor
   certification), lifesaving certification, teaching record, the Good Standing declaration,
   and a brief statement of intent.
2. Pay the course registration fee (the quantum is held in the Portal fee schedule; see §8).
3. Attend the full **Examiner Certification Course** (2–3 days, §5).
4. Meet the **pass requirements** (§6), including the pilot certification target.
5. Receive Coaching Panel ratification on the Chief Examiner's recommendation and
   Chairperson's endorsement.
6. Sign the **Examiner Code of Conduct** (Assessment System Handbook §7.1).

On ratification, the Portal grants the examiner role and generates the examiner's UID, after
which the examiner can be invited to sessions.

## 4. The Master Trainer

The **Master Trainer** is the Coaching-Panel-designated authority who develops and delivers
the certification courses. The role is unified — a single Master Trainer covers both the
instructor and examiner streams (OD-2); this Guide is the examiner stream. The role is
functional, operating in cycles tied to course intakes. Responsibilities for the examiner
stream:

- Co-develop the Examiner Certification Syllabus, methodologies, and assessment criteria
  with the Chief Examiner; submit to the Coaching Panel for approval and the Board for
  ratification.
- Plan, schedule, and deliver each course, classroom and poolside.
- Lead the practical assessment of candidate examiners and recommend approvals to the Chief
  Examiner.
- Manage procurement of certification kits (certificates and badges) so stock is available
  for issuance.
- Submit a course report to the Coaching Panel after every intake.
- Maintain course materials and refresh content in line with syllabus changes.

The inaugural course is co-attended by the Chief Examiner, so the methodology transfers and
the Chief Examiner qualifies under the syllabus they will steward. **Inaugural Master
Trainer: Mr. Melvin Chua**, holding both streams.

## 5. The course — format and modules

**Format.** 2–3 days; classroom briefing combined with practical poolside assessment of
mock candidates; class size capped to allow individual practical assessment of every
participant. Attendance at every module is **compulsory** — a missed module means repeating
the full course at the next intake. There are no partial completions.

| Mod. | Title | Hours | Mode | Content |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Program & Governance | 1.0 | Classroom | MAS structure (Board, Coaching Panel, Chairperson, Chief Examiner); the 7-level pathway; where the examiner fits; the BADGES document system. |
| 2 | Levels 1–3 Standards | 2.0 | Classroom + Pool | Every criterion for Starfish, Sea Turtle, Guppy; common observation pitfalls; competent vs. struggling performance. |
| 3 | Levels 4–5 Standards | 2.0 | Classroom + Pool | Every criterion for Octopus, Frog; stroke biomechanics cues; sit dive and squat dive standards. |
| 4 | Levels 6–7 Standards | 2.0 | Classroom + Pool | Every criterion for Swordfish, Dolphin; competition starts, turns, IM sequence; timing protocols for the L7 time qualifications. |
| 5 | Conduct of Assessment | 1.5 | Classroom + Role-play | Pre-assessment venue and safety check; candidate briefing; observation positioning and note-taking; Pass/Refer decisions and feedback; completing the assessment record. |
| 6 | Code of Conduct | 1.0 | Classroom | Conflict-of-interest scenarios; confidentiality; gifts and payments; standards discipline; breach procedures; safeguarding. |
| **7** | **BADGES Portal for Examiners** | **1.0** | **Hands-on** | **NEW.** Accepting session invitations; reading the assigned roster; recording Pass/Refer; how recording triggers pass-gated certificate issuance; what the examiner can and cannot see; the COI block in practice. |
| 8 | Practical Independent Assessment | 3.0 | Pool | Each candidate runs a real mock assessment under observation (eye-level analysis) by the Master Trainer, with the Chief Examiner present for the inaugural course. Direct feedback; final approval recommendation. |

*Approx. 13.5 hours of contact, across two to three days. (Module 7 is the
architecture-mandated Portal-competency addition; the original Appendix C numbering of the
practical as Module 7 becomes Module 8.)*

**Indicative day plan.** Day 1 — Modules 1–4. Day 2 — Modules 5–7 (conduct, code, and the
Portal). Day 3 — Module 8 independent practical assessment, final review, approval
recommendations.

## 6. Assessment and pass requirements

To be approved as a MAS Certified Examiner, a candidate must:

- **Attend every module in full.** Partial attendance is not accepted.
- **Pass the theory assessment** at the end of Modules 1–6 with a minimum score of **80%**.
- **Demonstrate Portal competency** in Module 7 — record a complete mock session correctly,
  including a Refer with reasons and a triggered certificate issuance.
- **Achieve Competent or above** on the practical assessment in Module 8, as judged by the
  Master Trainer or designated workshop leader.
- **Complete the Pilot Certification Target** — assess a minimum of **ten (10) candidates
  across at least three (3) different badge levels within six (6) months** of the course,
  under the Chief Examiner's oversight.
- **Sign the Examiner Code of Conduct.**
- **Receive Coaching Panel ratification** on the Chief Examiner's recommendation and the
  Chairperson's endorsement.

## 7. Term, renewal, and continuing certification

- **Initial appointment:** two (2) years from approval, aligned with the MAS governance term.
- **Continuing certification (good standing)** requires the holder to: conduct at least one
  assessment per twelve-month period (or notify leave); attend the joint annual meeting;
  maintain current **BADGES Instructor certification**; maintain current First Aid/CPR,
  Bronze Medallion, or equivalent lifesaving certification; pass periodic audits by the
  Chief Examiner; and complete any refresher modules issued by the Coaching Panel after
  syllabus updates.
- **Renewal** requires an active assessment record, satisfactory audits, annual-meeting
  attendance, and current Instructor and lifesaving certification.
- **Inactivity** (no assessments in 12 consecutive months without notified leave) triggers a
  standing review. **Audit failure** may lead to supervised assessment status, retraining at
  the next course, or removal — at the Coaching Panel's discretion on the Chief Examiner's
  recommendation with the Chairperson's endorsement.

## 8. Fees

The course registration fee and its partial-refund-on-pilot-completion incentive are set on
the single national schedule and held in the **Portal fee schedule** (seeded from Manual
§16). Up to 50% of the registration fee is refundable once the examiner completes the pilot
certification target; the non-refunded portion covers administration, materials, and
certificate stock.

## 9. Founding cohort calibration

Because the founding cohort has no prior body of examiners to calibrate against, the
inaugural course includes an **extended Module 8** in which all candidates assess the same
set of mock candidates. The Chief Examiner and Coaching Panel members present compare ratings
to confirm inter-examiner consistency before approving the cohort. Any candidate whose
ratings deviate substantially from consensus repeats Module 8 at the next intake before
approval.

---

## 10. On the BADGES Platform Portal

The examiner's entire operational life runs through the Portal, which is why Portal
competency is a certified outcome (Module 7), not an afterthought:

- **Application & UID** — the examiner application is submitted in the Portal; on
  ratification the examiner role is granted and a UID auto-generated.
- **Session invitations** — the examiner accepts or declines invitations; accepting assigns
  the roster and makes those candidates gradeable by that examiner and no other.
- **Recording** — Pass/Refer is recorded per candidate per level; a Refer carries reasons;
  the COI trigger blocks any candidate the examiner instructed.
- **Certification** — a recorded Pass auto-links to a serialized, append-only certificate;
  verification is anonymized.
