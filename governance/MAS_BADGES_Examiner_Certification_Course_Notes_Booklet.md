**How to use these notes**

These notes follow the examiner course module by module. Each module
page gives you the outcome (what you'll be able to do), the key points,
and space for your own notes. Write in them freely — a good examiner
keeps a working notebook.

The notes mirror the Assessment System Handbook (the standard) and the
Examiner Certification Course Guide (the course). Where the two go
deeper, this workbook points you there.

**Poolside calibration**

Your judgement, checked against the Chief Examiner and the Master
Trainer. All three of you watch the same swimmer against the same
criterion; a Pass/Refer call is calibrated when the three of you agree.
This is what the practical (Module 9) tests, and what keeps the standard
consistent across every examiner in the country.

> **You will be able to:** distinguish Competent from Struggling

**Key points**

| **M3** | **Levels 4–5 Standards** | CLASSROOM + POOL |

> **You will be able to:** distinguish Competent from Struggling
> performance at Octopus and Frog, including dive and stroke
> biomechanics.

**Key points**

> **•** L4 Octopus: FC bilateral 15 m, BK 15 m, breaststroke whip kick
> 10 m, sit dive + 3 m underwater glide, survival BK 10 m.
>
> **•** L5 Frog: FC/BK/BR 25 m each, dolphin kick 10 m,
> sculling+flutter+breath 15 m, flip turns both ways, squat dive + 5 m
> dolphin underwater.
>
> **•** The screw kick is the classic breaststroke fault — observe from
> behind, both heels must turn out symmetrically.
>
> **•** Dive judgement is about the entry line and streamline
> underwater, not the height of the entry.

| **M4** | **Levels 6–7 Standards** | CLASSROOM + POOL |

> **You will be able to:** distinguish Competent from Struggling at
> Swordfish and Dolphin, and apply timing at L7.

**Key points**

> **•** L6 Swordfish (deep water): 50 m each FC/BK/BR-with-pullout; 10 m

>
> **•** L7 is the only level with a time gate — bring a stopwatch. A
> stroke or a time missing is a Refer.

Shallow water · Max 16 per session

|                                                                  |                                                                                                      |                                                                                                                    |
|------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CRITERION**                                                    | **COMPETENT ✓ (Pass)**                                                                               | **STRUGGLING (Refer)**                                                                                             |
| **1 Enter and exit the pool safely.**                            | Controlled entry (steps, seated, or supported); calm, purposeful; safe exit using edge / ladder.     | Jumps/runs in; visible fear; needs lifting out; no awareness of pool rules.                                        |
| **2 Bobbing — 10 reps, full submersion, exhale + inhale cycle.** | Rhythmic, calm; head fully submerged each time; clean exhale then inhale; 10 clean reps in sequence. | Gasps at surface; incomplete submersion (nose/mouth stay out); breaks before 10; holds breath rather than exhales. |
| **3 Front float — 10 seconds.**                                  | Horizontal, face-down, arms out, relaxed body; unassisted; stable for the full 10 s.                 | Hips sink below 45°; lifts head early; needs support; unable to stay 10 s.                                         |
| **4 Back float — 10 seconds.**                                   | Hips up, ears in, calm face; unassisted; stable for the full 10 s.                                   | Piked position (hips low); hands grabbing air; head tucked forward; sits rather than floats.                       |
| **5 Streamlined front flutter kick — 3 metres.**                 | Streamline hold; small fast kick from the hips; ankles loose; clear forward travel; 3 m reached.     | Big knee-bend bicycling; feet flexed / dorsiflexed; no forward travel; stops repeatedly.                           |
| **6 Back push and glide — 2 metres.**                            | Locked streamline; clean push off wall; 2 m reached before recovery.                                 | Arms not locked; sinks immediately; no push off wall; less than 2 m.                                               |
| **7 Lateral rotation — both directions.**                        | Controlled roll front→back and back→front, unassisted, both directions.                              | Flails; only one direction; needs support; loses horizontal position.                                              |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** Buoyant children can appear to float — check for real body
> control, not just being held up by fat/lung volume.
>
> **⚠** Kids trained to bob may hold breath rather than exhale — watch
> for bubbles at the surface.
>
> **⚠** Count 10 seconds silently to yourself — don't judge by feel.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow water. All skills done independently. Order of the
> day is: enter → bobs → front float (roll) → back float (roll) →
> flutter → back glide → exit.

Shallow water · Max 14 per session

|                                                                    |                                                                                                             |                                                                                                                |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CRITERION**                                                      | **COMPETENT ✓ (Pass)**                                                                                      | **STRUGGLING (Refer)**                                                                                         |
| **1 Fall in, turn, grab wall.**                                    | Intentional entry (loss of balance-style); controlled rotation in the water; purposeful grab of wall/ledge. | Deliberate confident dive rather than 'fall'; no rotation; misses the wall; needs prompt.                      |
| **2 Basic front crawl arm movement with flutter kick — 5 metres.** | Recognisable alternating arm action; some head control; clear forward progress; 5 m reached.                | Arms slap water without pull; head submerged whole time or head fully up; no forward travel; halts before 5 m. |
| **3 Back flutter kick — 5 metres.**                                | Body flat; hips up; kick from the hips; sustained forward progress; 5 m clear.                              | Sitting position; kick from the knee; no distance; head tucked forward.                                        |
| **4 Basic backstroke arm movement with flutter kick — 5 metres.**  | Recognisable alternating arm action; back position held; 5 m reached.                                       | Head in water; sitting/piked position; arms flapping without pull; no distance.                                |
| **5 Reach and pull with comfortable breath — 5 metres.**           | Sculling / reach motion visible; breath maintained throughout; forward progress; 5 m clear.                 | Panic breath; drowning-style movements; no forward progress; hands stiff.                                      |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** 'Fall in' is not a dive — needs to feel like a controlled loss
> of position. A confident dive is a Refer on this criterion.
>
> **⚠** Arm-mechanics are basic at this level — do not judge to a
> full-stroke standard; look for the pattern.
>
> **⚠** Watch that the swimmer is breathing throughout reach-and-pull,
> not holding breath the whole 5 m.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow water. All skills done independently.
> Fall-in-turn-grab is the survival criterion — non-negotiable for L2.

Shallow or deep water · Max 12 per session

|                                                          |                                                                                                        |                                                                                                                  |
|----------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CRITERION**                                            | **COMPETENT ✓ (Pass)**                                                                                 | **STRUGGLING (Refer)**                                                                                           |
| **1 Front crawl with unilateral breathing — 10 metres.** | Rhythmic breathing to one consistent side; head turns (not lifts); stroke stays connected; 10 m clear. | Head-up breathing (lifts rather than turns); loses rhythm at breath; head-down and holds breath for 10 m; halts. |
| **2 Backstroke — 10 metres.**                            | Hips up; ears in the water; alternating pull; 10 m clear.                                              | Sits or pikes; veers into lane line; halts; head lifted.                                                         |
| **3 Streamlined underwater push and glide — 3 metres.**  | Clean push off wall; body streamlined and submerged; 3 m reached before surfacing.                     | Surfaces early; streamline unlocked (arms apart); no clean push; travels less than 3 m.                          |
| **4 Water treading — 30 seconds.**                       | Head consistently above water; controlled sculling/kick; relaxed; 30 s clear.                          | Dips below the surface; uses wall/lane rope; gasps; unable to sustain 30 s.                                      |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** Unilateral breathing is often taught as a head-lift — a lift is
> a Refer. Insist on the turn.
>
> **⚠** Underwater push-and-glide is a passive glide from a push — not a
> swum underwater length. Watch that the swimmer is not kicking or
> pulling.
>
> **⚠** Deep water may be the candidate's first experience — reassure
> between skills; observe from a safe position.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow or deep water. Treading is best done in deep water.
> All skills independent.

Shallow or deep water · Max 10 per session

|                                                          |                                                                                                          |                                                                                                                 |
|----------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CRITERION**                                            | **COMPETENT ✓ (Pass)**                                                                                   | **STRUGGLING (Refer)**                                                                                          |
| **1 Front crawl with bilateral breathing — 15 metres.**  | Alternates breathing sides consistently; rhythm held over the full 15 m; head rotates cleanly.           | One-side dominant with token attempts at the other side; loses rhythm; chokes at the weaker-side breath; halts. |
| **2 Backstroke — 15 metres.**                            | Efficient stroke; hips up; consistent alternating pull; 15 m clear.                                      | Sitting position; inconsistent arm cycle; halts; veers.                                                         |
| **3 Breaststroke whip kick — 10 metres.**                | Symmetric: heels to bottom → turn out → whip and squeeze together; visible forward progress; 10 m clear. | Screw kick (one heel higher than the other, asymmetric turn-out); no forward travel; frog-kick without whip.    |
| **4 Sit dive + underwater streamline glide — 3 metres.** | Controlled entry (no belly flop); chin tucked; streamlined body underwater; 3 m reached before surface.  | Belly flop; feet-first entry; surfaces immediately; less than 3 m.                                              |
| **5 Survival backstroke — 10 metres.**                   | Hips up; minimum arm/leg action; breath in control; sustained 10 m.                                      | Head high; over-active arms (wasting energy); panicked pace; no distance.                                       |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** Screw kick is masked by forward momentum from the pull — observe
> from behind, or from directly overhead.
>
> **⚠** Bilateral breathing sometimes shows as 3-stroke-one-side then a
> token 3-stroke-other-side — insist on true alternating over the 15 m.
>
> **⚠** Sit dive: chin tuck and arm lock matter more than height off the
> deck.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow or deep water. Sit dive is from the wet deck, not
> from blocks.

Shallow or deep water · Max 8 per session

|                                                             |                                                                                                                 |                                                                                             |
|-------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CRITERION**                                               | **COMPETENT ✓ (Pass)**                                                                                          | **STRUGGLING (Refer)**                                                                      |
| **1 Front crawl with bilateral breathing — 25 metres.**     | Sustained rhythm and bilateral breathing across the full 25 m; no walking between reps; stroke stays connected. | Walks / touches down mid-length; loses bilateral in the last 5 m; chokes; stops for breath. |
| **2 Backstroke — 25 metres.**                               | Hips-up position held; no head lift; consistent alternating pull; 25 m clear.                                   | Sitting position; head lifts; veers.                                                        |
| **3 Breaststroke — 25 metres.**                             | Pull-breathe-kick-glide timing intact; symmetric whip kick; visible glide phase between strokes.                | No glide phase; rushed timing; screw kick; asymmetric heel action.                          |
| **4 Dolphin kick — 10 metres.**                             | Hip-led undulation; both legs move together; clear forward travel; 10 m reached.                                | Flutter-with-dolphin-attempt (single-leg lead); knee-only movement; no undulation.          |
| **5 Sculling with flutter kick and breathing — 15 metres.** | Figure-8 scull with wrists tilted; flutter kick underneath; breath every few strokes; 15 m clear.               | Stiff wrists (no sculling motion); flutter fails; breath lost; halts.                       |
| **6 Flip turn — forwards and backwards separately.**        | Clear rotation off the wall; streamlined push-off with underwater kicks; both directions independently.         | No rotation (walks off wall); no push-off; no streamline; fails one direction.              |
| **7 Squat dive + underwater dolphin — 5 metres.**           | Entry angle correct (fingertip lead); streamlined; dolphin kick sustained underwater to 5 m.                    | Belly flop; surfaces early; no dolphin kick; less than 5 m.                                 |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** 'Attempted' flip turn is not competent — look for the
> streamlined push-off distance, not just the tumble.
>
> **⚠** 25 m distances tax stroke integrity — signs of struggle appear
> in the last 5 m; observe the finish especially.
>
> **⚠** Dolphin kick is often single-plane and knee-driven — insist on
> true hip lead. Two-body-wave should be visible.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow or deep water. Squat dive is from the wet deck. Flip
> turns forwards AND backwards — both required.

| **1 Front crawl — 50 metres with tumble turns.**                     | Sustained stroke across all four lengths; tumble turn at each wall; pace does not collapse.             | Wall-touch turn rather than tumble; pace degrades badly on later lengths; walks touchdown at a wall.                      |
| **2 Backstroke — 50 metres.**                                        | Hips up; competitive-style turn at each wall; 50 m clear.                                               | Sits; wall-grab-only at turn; halts; veers.                                                                               |
| **3 Breaststroke with underwater pullout — 50 metres (open turns).** | Legal pull-out off each wall (one full pull, glide); symmetric whip kick; open turn correctly executed. | Pull-out skipped or half-done (breath at surface immediately); screw kick; incorrect open turn (both hands not touching). |
| **4 Butterfly — 10 metres.**                                         | Hip-led undulation; two kicks per pull; arm recovery clear of the water; sustained for 10 m.            | Arms drag / don't clear the water; single-plane knee-driven kick; stalls before 10 m; degrades to survival stroke.        |

| **7 Stand dive + underwater dolphin — 5 metres.**                    | Controlled competition-style entry; streamlined; 5 m of dolphin kick before surface.                    | Belly flop; surfaces before 5 m; no dolphin kicks.                                                                        |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** The breaststroke pullout is often skipped or half-done — must be
> a full pull under water. If breath surfaces immediately, that's a
> Refer.
>
> **⚠** 10 m butterfly is often 'attempted' — insist on legal butterfly
> for the full 10 m. Arms drag = Refer.
>
> **⚠** Tumble turn (for FC) vs. open turn (for BR/fly) — know which
> criterion applies to which stroke.
>
> **⚠** Stand dive at L6 is safe-and-streamlined; at L7 it becomes
> competitive-style off blocks.

**Procedural notes**

> Assess in shallow or deep water.

| **6 Open turn in and out with underwater kicks.**                   | Legal open turn (both hands touch); pushed streamline; underwater kicks.                            | Not legal; only one hand touches; no push; no kicks.                        |
| **7 Stand dive off blocks / wet deck with underwater skills.**      | Competitive-form entry; streamlined; skill (dolphin or flutter) into stroke.                        | Safe but not competitive; belly-flop; no streamline; no underwater skills.  |
| **8 Backstroke dive off blocks / wet deck with underwater skills.** | Legal backstroke start; arch and streamline; underwater dolphin kicks; correct emergence to stroke. | Slips at start; no arch; no dolphin kicks; illegal recovery.                |
| **9 Individual Medley — 100 m within 3:00.**                        | Correct order (fly → BK → BR → FC); legal transitions at each wall; within 3:00.                    | Wrong stroke order; illegal transition; misses time; one stroke fails.      |

**Observation pitfalls at this level**

> **⚠** L7 is time-critical — bring a stopwatch. Time is a hard
> criterion, not a guideline.
>
> **⚠** Do not accept 'attempted' for any stroke at this level. If one
> stroke fails, the level is Referred.
>
> **⚠** IM transitions have specific legal requirements — observe each
> wall carefully.
>
> **⚠** Backstroke dive off blocks is a specialised entry — check the
> arch and streamline separately from the kicks.

**Procedural notes**

> Deep water only. L7 is a competitive-bridge level: judge to World
> Aquatics standards for legal strokes and turns. Two hard timings: FC
> 100 m ≤ 2:30 and IM 100 m ≤ 3:00.
