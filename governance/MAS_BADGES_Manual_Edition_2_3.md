PART I — FOUNDATIONS

1\. Introduction & scope of this Manual

This Manual is the constitution of the MAS BADGES programme — Malaysia
Aquatics' national seven-level Learn-to-Swim certification (Starfish →
Dolphin). It states the programme's vision, who is accountable to whom,
and what the system **requires** on each topic; it does not state
**how** each requirement is met. The "how" lives in the specialist
documents and is enforced in the Portal (§3). Read this Manual for the
requirements and the chain of accountability, and follow its
cross-references to the Syllabus, Handbooks, and Course Guides for
method and detail.

2\. Vision, mission & objectives

**Vision.** A trusted national Learn-to-Swim standard that carries a
child from first confidence in the water to the threshold of competitive
aquatics.

**Mission.** To certify swimmers against a published seven-level
standard through credible, independent assessment, supported by
certified instructors and recognised partner centres.

**Objectives.** The programme exists to:

- Establish and maintain a national seven-level proficiency standard
  (Starfish → Dolphin).

- Certify and support a pool of BADGES Instructors who prepare
  candidates to the standard.

- Recognise swim schools, clubs, and academies as Official BADGES
  Partner Centres.

- Deliver a credible, transparent assessment that schools, parents, and
  competition

selectors can trust.

- Provide a clear progression bridge from Learn-to-Swim into competitive
  aquatics.

3\. The governing document architecture

The BADGES system is governed by a layered set of documents and one
execution hub. This Manual states requirements; the specialist documents
carry the specifics; the Portal enforces.

| **Document**                              | **Owns**                                                                 | **Custodian**                                                         |
|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **This Manual**                           | Vision, governance, accountability, and the *requirements* of the system | Chairperson (program owner); Coaching Panel co-owns technical clauses |
| **7-Level Syllabus**                      | The proficiency standard — criteria/outcomes L1–L7                       | Coaching & Technical Board                                            |
| **Teaching System Handbook**              | How instruction is delivered to the standard                             | Coaching Panel + Master Trainer                                       |
| **Instructor Certification Course Guide** | How a person is certified to instruct                                    | BADGES System Master Trainer                                          |
| **Assessment System Handbook**            | How levels are assessed, recorded, certified                             | Chief Examiner                                                        |
| **Examiner Certification Course Guide**   | How a person is certified to assess                                      | Master Trainer, with the Chief Examiner                               |
| **BADGES Platform Portal**                | The system of record that executes the policies                          | System administrator; governance via access controls                  |

**Precedence.** Where this Manual and a specialist document appear to
differ on *policy*, this Manual governs and the specialist document is
corrected. Where a document and the **Portal** differ on an *operational
value or state* (a fee, a threshold, a session status), the Portal's
configured value is authoritative for operations and the document is
corrected. One fact has one home.

**Change control.** Standard and technical changes (criteria, level
definitions, examiner-syllabus design) are proposed by the Chief
Examiner or Chairperson, approved by the Coaching Panel, and ratified by
the Board. Operational details within a Handbook may be revised by its
custodian with the Chairperson's endorsement **without re-ratifying this
Manual** — this is what keeps the Manual stable and the Handbooks
current.

4\. Definitions & glossary

Key terms: **Candidate** (a swimmer being prepared for or assessed at a
level); **Instructor** (a BADGES-certified teacher who prepares
candidates); **Examiner** (a BADGES-certified assessor); **Master
Trainer** (the authority who delivers the certification courses);
**Partner Centre** (a recognized swim entity); **Pass / Refer** (the two
assessment outcomes); **the Portal** (apps.masbadges.org). A fuller
glossary is maintained as Appendix C.

PART II — GOVERNANCE & ACCOUNTABILITY

5\. Governance & reporting structure

The program is governed by **two parallel authorities under the MAS
Board**, working in coordination:

- The **Coaching & Technical Board (Coaching Panel)** — the standing
  technical authority.

It owns the Syllabus and technical standards and ratifies examiner
appointments.

- The **BADGES Chairperson** — the program owner. The Chairperson runs
  rollout, instructor

liaison, the examiner pool, partnerships, and budget.

The two are **peers under the Board, not stacked**: technical decisions
flow through the Coaching Panel, operational decisions through the
Chairperson, with the Board as final authority on either.

**Chain of accountability:** MAS Board → (Coaching & Technical Board
*and* BADGES Chairperson, as peers) → Chief Examiner (technical to the
Coaching Panel; operational to the Chairperson) → Approved Examiners →
Partner Centres and Certified Instructors.

**Decision routing (the working rulebook):**

| **Decision**                                 | **Routing**                                                                         |
|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Syllabus / technical criteria change         | CE or Chairperson proposes → Coaching Panel approves → Board ratifies               |
| Certification-course syllabus design         | Master Trainer + CE co-design → Coaching Panel approves → Board ratifies            |
| Examiner appointment                         | CE recommends → Chairperson endorses → Coaching Panel ratifies → Board confirms     |
| Chief Examiner appointment                   | Chairperson nominates with Coaching Panel → Board approves                          |
| Partner Centre recognition                   | Chairperson approves on application → notifies Board                                |
| Fees, allowance schedule, scheduling         | Chairperson with CE → Board approves the schedule                                   |
| Audit findings / technical disputes          | CE → Coaching Panel                                                                 |
| Operational disputes / Partner Centre issues | CE or partner → Chairperson                                                         |
| Examiner discipline or removal               | CE reports → Chairperson endorses → Coaching Panel decides → Board ratifies removal |
| Bi-annual program report                     | CE submits to Chairperson (operational) and Coaching Panel (technical)              |

6\. The Chairperson

**Requirement.** The program has a Chairperson, appointed by the Board
in consultation with the Coaching Panel, who is the named accountable
program owner: rollout and administration, instructor-pool liaison, the
Partner Centre register, endorsement of examiner appointments, the
program budget and proposed fee schedule, and the public-facing
representation of the program. The Chairperson recommends — but does not
unilaterally amend — syllabus content; technical content always goes to
the Coaching Panel.

**Eligibility (in brief).** A senior MAS-affiliated practitioner or
sitting Board/Coaching Panel member; administrative capacity; Good
Standing; no compromising conflict with any single Partner Centre
(recusal where personal involvement exists).

**Term.** Two years, aligned with the MAS governance term, renewable by
the Board with the Coaching Panel. *Inaugural appointment: Ms. Nancy Yap
(honorary).*

7\. The Chief Examiner

**Requirement.** The program has a Chief Examiner, nominated by the
Chairperson with the Coaching Panel and approved by the Board, who is
the operational head of the examiner pool — query resolution, examiner
welfare and scheduling, the national Examiner Register, quality audits,
cross-state coordination, the bi-annual report, and appointment
recommendations. The Chief Examiner reports operationally to the
Chairperson and technically to the Coaching Panel, **holds custody of
the Assessment System Handbook**, and may serve as a working examiner.

**Eligibility (in brief).** A current Examiner with two years'
experience, or a senior coach/instructor who has led the examiner
course; current Instructor and Examiner certification; lifesaving
certification; administrative competence; Good Standing; no compromising
commercial conflict (does not examine candidates they instruct — see
§9).

**Term.** Two years, renewable. *Inaugural appointment: Ms. Clara Chung
(voluntary, founding phase), under the Founding Phase Provision in §18.*

8\. The BADGES System Master Trainer

**Requirement.** The program has a **Master Trainer** — a
Coaching-Panel-designated authority who develops, with the Chief
Examiner, the certification-course syllabi and delivers the **Instructor
Certification** and **Examiner Certification** courses. The title is
"Master Trainer" because the role trains **both instructors and
examiners**. The role is functional, operating in cycles tied to course
intakes, and includes procurement of certification kits and a course
report to the Coaching Panel after each intake.

The role is **unified**: a single Master Trainer covers both courses.
**Inaugural Master Trainer: Mr. Melvin Chua**, holding both streams.
Additional Master Trainers may be appointed; where more than one serves,
one holder (or the Coaching Panel) is the **curriculum custodian**, and
separate intakes **calibrate against each other** so all trainers
certify to one bar. *(OD-2 resolved — Governance Decisions Register.)*

9\. Code of conduct & child safety

**Requirement.** Every examiner and instructor signs and upholds a code
of conduct, of which the non-negotiable elements are: the
**conflict-of-interest rule** (no one assesses a candidate they
instruct); no gifts or payments beyond official fees; standards
discipline (an award is never lowered); confidentiality of candidate
data; and **child safety / safeguarding** as a hard floor — parental
consent before participation, no one-to-one isolation of a child out of
sight, no public disclosure of a minor's identity, and immediate
escalation of concerns.

**Affiliation and recusal.** The Master Trainer and Chief Examiner **may
hold a centre affiliation and may have students**; the program does not
bar this, because the two material conflicts are blocked at source —
**centre recognition rests solely with the Chairperson**, and **no
examiner may assess a swimmer they instruct** (enforced in the Portal).
Residual proximity is handled by **recusal**: the Chief Examiner does
not approve their own centre's assessment venue, nor act on scheduling
or complaints in which their own centre is the subject; and
certification of the Master Trainer's own-centre staff is sighted by the
Coaching Panel. *(COI-1 resolved — Governance Decisions Register.)*

**Designated Safeguarding Lead.** MAS maintains a Designated
Safeguarding Lead to whom safeguarding concerns are escalated. Pending a
dedicated appointment, the **Chairperson serves as interim Designated
Safeguarding Lead**. *(SG-1 resolved.)*

**Specifics deferred.** The operating **Examiner Code of Conduct** is
set out in the Assessment System Handbook (§7.1); the instructor conduct
undertaking is in the Instructor Certification Course Guide.
*(Reconciliation R5: the full operating code lives in the Handbook; this
Manual retains only the requirement.)*

PART III — SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

*This Part states the requirement on each topic and the instrument that
carries the specifics. The requirements-to-instrument matrix in §14
summarizes the whole.*

10\. The 7-level certification

**Requirement.** BADGES certification consists of seven progressive
levels, each awarded on independent demonstration of a published
standard, assessed by a certified examiner and recorded as Pass or
Refer, with no partial pass and no skipping of levels. **Specifics
deferred** to the **7-Level Syllabus**, which owns all criteria and the
progression pathway.

11\. Instruction & instructor certification

**Requirement.** Instruction is delivered only by **BADGES-certified
instructors**, using the published teaching system, to bring candidates
genuinely to the Syllabus standard. Instructors prepare candidates and
book assessments but never assess their own candidates.

The **BADGES Instructor certification** is the single, current
instructor credential and the national standard. A prior MAS instructor
certification is **recognised for standing** but is **not listed on the
public instructor directory** until the holder gains the BADGES
Instructor certification — obtainable through the course as a
**discounted refresher** — because the syllabus content is materially
new. **Specifics deferred** to the **Teaching System Handbook** (method)
and the **Instructor Certification Course Guide** (certification),
executed through the Portal. *(OD-1 resolved.)*

12\. Assessment & examiner certification

**Requirement.** Assessment is **credible, independent, auditable, and
anonymized**: conducted by a certified examiner who did not instruct the
candidate, recorded contemporaneously, retained as progression history,
and verifiable publicly without ever exposing a minor's identity.
Examiners are certified before they assess and hold a **current BADGES
Instructor certification** as a prerequisite.

**Founding-cohort exception.** For the founding cohort, a recognised
legacy instructor certification is accepted to enter the examiner
course; the course delivers the new rubrics and the poolside
calibration, and completing it **confers BADGES Instructor standing** —
so a founding examiner is directory-listed without a separate instructor
refresher. *(OD-1a resolved.)* **Specifics deferred** to the
**Assessment System Handbook** (procedure; custody: Chief Examiner) and
the **Examiner Certification Course Guide** (certification), executed
through the Portal.

13\. Partner Centres

**Requirement.** Swim entities may be recognized as **Official BADGES
Partner Centres** against published criteria — legal status, suitable
pool access, at least one active MAS-certified instructor, a safety and
child-protection baseline, current public-liability insurance, Good
Standing, and a commitment to deliver the Syllabus and route assessments
through the MAS process. Recognition confers privileges (the mark, the
public directory listing, hosting rights) and obligations (route all
assessments through MAS, cooperate with audits, maintain standards,
submit the annual return). A swim entity need not be a Partner Centre to
submit candidates — direct instructor submissions remain open.

**Requirements retained here:** recognition criteria, the
privileges/obligations principle, suspension and removal grounds, the
renewal principle (annual return; from 1 January 2027 a minimum
50-candidate annual threshold; founding-cohort grace per §18).
**Operational specifics deferred** to the Portal (application,
directory, annual-return mechanics, status lifecycle). Centre
requirements **remain in this Manual**; no dedicated Partner Centre
Guide is adopted at this stage, and one will be created only if centre
operations warrant it. *(OD-3 resolved.)*

14\. The BADGES Platform Portal — system of record

**Requirement.** The Portal (apps.masbadges.org) is the system of record
and the execution hub. Registration, claim, booking, assessment
recording, certification, public verification, billing, and the public
directories are transacted through it. Its **configured operational
values are authoritative**. Public certificate verification is
**anonymized and never exposes a minor's identity** — a hard
child-safety requirement enforced in the Portal, not merely stated here.
The public website (www.masbadges.org) consumes only curated, read-only
published views; the portal/website boundary is never crossed.

15\. Records, data protection & child safety

**Requirement.** Candidate records are confidential and used only for
MAS certification purposes. **Malaysia Aquatics is the data controller;
Partner Centres act as processors** *(governance wording subject to
legal counsel)*. A minor's name and identifying information are never
exposed in public verification or any public listing. Progression
history is retained to support accurate certification and readiness
judgements.

16\. Fees & commercial framework

**Requirement.** The program operates a **single national fee schedule**
covering student assessment fees, the Partner Centre application and
annual recognition fees, the certification-course registration fee, and
the cross-state deployment allowance. Fees are proposed by the
Chairperson and **approved by the Board**, reviewed annually. **Quanta
deferred** to the Portal fee schedule, which is authoritative *(seeded
from the v1.4 figures; reconciliation R3)*.

**MAS as payment intermediary (R2, confirmed).** MAS collects assessment
fees from the requesting party and remunerates examiners from those fees
per the Board-approved payout schedule. Every assessment session carries
two settlement legs — payment in from the requesting party and payout
out to the examiner — and does not close until both are settled and
recorded. Cross-state deployment allowances likewise route through MAS
for auditability. The mechanics are set out in the Assessment System
Handbook (§2.3, §3.6) and Manual §14.5–§14.6.

PART IV — PROGRAM LIFECYCLE

17\. Pilot phase & national rollout

The program rolls out in two phases. The **pilot** appoints the
inaugural Chairperson and Chief Examiner under the Founding Phase
Provisions, publishes the certification syllabi, certifies the first
cohorts of instructors and examiners, conducts the first assessments
across all seven levels, recognizes the first Partner Centres, and
validates the criteria, course content, procedures, and audit process.
On the Coaching Panel's and Board's satisfaction, the program moves to
**national rollout**: the framework is unchanged; what scales is volume
— more courses, more state examiners up to the published quotas, more
Partner Centres, a larger pipeline. The Founding Phase Provisions then
sunset.

18\. State allocation & founding provisions

**Requirement.** Examiners are appointed against **state quotas** to
balance demand-density with national coverage; the pilot fixes a
national cohort, adjustable by the Coaching Panel on the Chief
Examiner's recommendation and Chairperson's endorsement. Where a state
is short, the **cross-state deployment** mechanism applies. **The quota
schedule and allowance quanta are deferred** to the Portal/governance
schedule; the cross-state procedure is in the Assessment System Handbook
(§15).

**Founding Phase Provisions (summary).** The inaugural Chief Examiner's
prior-examining and Examiner-certification requirements are waived
(co-developer of, and first to qualify under, the examiner syllabus);
the founding examiner cohort is certified through the inaugural course;
Partner Centre founding-cohort applications are fast-tracked
(documentary review only), the recognition fee is set at 50% prorated to
31 December 2026, and the 50-candidate renewal threshold takes effect
only from 1 January 2027. Each provision sunsets when the Coaching Panel
determines a stable pool is established.

19\. Expected outcomes & review cycle

**Expected outcomes.** A recognised national standard; a growing pool of
certified instructors and examiners; a network of recognised Partner
Centres; and a transparent, auditable certification trusted by families
and competition selectors.

**Review.** The program is reviewed on the MAS two-year governance
cycle. The Chief Examiner's bi-annual report and the Coaching Panel's
technical review inform revisions, routed through the change-control
process in §3 and §5.

**Appendix D — Living Decisions & Open Items Register**

This register is the single index of all committee decisions and open
items across the BADGES document set. Items below handbook level (course
guides, notes booklets) never carry living or provisional content — they
are updated only when a decision here is marked resolved and the settled
value is written into the relevant handbook.

**Committee decisions**

**CD-1 · No result-appeal process**

Status: Settled. Decided by the Committee.

The BADGES programme does not operate a result-appeal process. The
recourse for a Refer is to re-prepare and re-attempt at any future
session, with no penalty and no limit on attempts. A concern about how
an assessment was conducted (e.g. an undeclared conflict of interest) is
a Code of Conduct matter referred in writing to the Chief Examiner
(Assessment System Handbook §3.8).

Document: Assessment System Handbook §3.8.

**CD-2 · Transitional swimmers — previous syllabus**

Status: Living / provisional. Approved by the Committee on 4 July 2026.

Swimmers holding certifications under the previous MAS syllabus register
for the equivalent new-syllabus level number, are assessed against the
new criteria, and pay only the fee for the highest level awarded in that
session. Certificates are issued only for levels actually assessed under
the new Syllabus. The operational procedure is in the Assessment System
Handbook, Annex F.

**Open questions:**

• Is the level-number equivalence automatic (previous Level N = new
Level N), or does the instructor make a judgement call on the
appropriate registration level?

• Does this provision have an expiry window (e.g. 12 months from
new-syllabus launch)?

• Should the Portal enforce the fee rule automatically (e.g. a "previous
syllabus" flag on registration), or is it handled manually by the
Finance Officer?

Document: Assessment System Handbook, Annex F.

**CD-3 · Swimmer on-paper knowledge check**

Status: Deferred (living). Registered 7 July 2026.

A future on-paper knowledge check for swimmers (pool rules, water
safety), examiner-administered, may be added as a complement to the
practical assessment. It is not part of the current programme. This item
is held here so the Assessment System Handbook can state the settled
current position (practical only) without carrying deferred language.
Distinct from the examiner-certification theory assessment, which lives
in the Examiner Certification Course Guide.

Open question: whether the check is adopted at all, and if so at which
levels and with what weight (advisory vs gating).

Document: Assessment System Handbook §4 (currently states practical
only).
