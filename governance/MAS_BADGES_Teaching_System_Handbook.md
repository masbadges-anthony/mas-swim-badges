**About this Handbook**

This Handbook is the **teaching doctrine** of the BADGES system — how a
certified instructor prepares a swimmer to meet the Syllabus. Its place
in the document set is precise:

• It **teaches toward** the **7-Level Syllabus** standard and never
restates the assessment criteria. The Syllabus owns the criteria; this
Handbook owns the *method*.

• It is the content the **Instructor Certification Course Guide** trains
against — every requirement here is taught, practised, and assessed
there.

• It runs alongside, but kept separate from, assessment: an instructor
prepares; a different, independent examiner judges.

• The instructor's administrative work is **executed in the BADGES
Platform Portal**.

**How this Handbook reads.** Part I is the doctrine. Part II lists, as
short **requirements**, what an instructor must learn — the Course Guide
is where each is taught. Part III covers the system. The annexes carry
the working tools: per-level focus, drills, correction cues, ratios, and
session plans.

**Custody.** The Coaching & Technical Board owns the teaching doctrine;
the Master Trainer maintains the delivery materials. Changes to method
follow the Coaching Panel route; changes to the underlying standard are
Syllabus changes, made there.

**PART I — TEACHING DOCTRINE**

**1. Teaching philosophy**

BADGES teaching produces swimmers who are **safe, competent, and
confident**, in that order of priority, along one continuous pathway
from a child's first day in the water to competitive readiness. Four
commitments hold it together:

• **Safety is the foundation, not a topic.** Water safety and
self-rescue run through every level, not taught once.

• **Competency over time-in-programme.** A swimmer advances when they
consistently meet a level's outcomes — at their own pace — never by
attendance or age alone.

• **Teach to the skill, not to the test.** Instructors build genuine,
transferable technique; passing is the by-product of real competence.

• **The instructor prepares; an independent examiner judges.** This
separation makes the badge credible, and instructors uphold it actively.

**2. The Introduce → Reinforce → Develop (IRD) model**

Every level is taught through the same three-phase cycle, already
embedded in the Syllabus's teaching objectives:

• **Introduce** — present a new skill in its simplest safe form; build
familiarity and confidence; accept rough execution.

• **Reinforce** — repeat and stabilise the skill across sessions until
it is consistent and unprompted.

• **Develop** — extend the skill toward the Syllabus standard: add
distance, efficiency, independence, and combination with other skills.

A swimmer is put forward for assessment only when every skill at the
level has moved through **Develop** and is performed **independently**
to the Syllabus criteria. Annex A sets the IRD focus per level; the
drill library (B1) and correction cues (B2) are the working tools.

**3. Teaching to the criteria — the discipline**

The Syllabus's right-hand column is the standard; the instructor builds
the underlying competence so meeting it is natural. Two disciplines
protect this:

• **No teaching to the test.** Instructors don't drill the exact
assessment task in isolation to manufacture a pass; they build the skill
so the task is comfortably within reach.

• **Honest readiness.** A swimmer is put forward only when genuinely
ready. A **Refer** is a normal, useful outcome — it names a gap to close
— never a failure of the instructor or a reason to pressure an examiner.

**PART II — INSTRUCTOR COMPETENCY REQUIREMENTS**

*What a certified instructor must be able to do. Each requirement is
taught in the Instructor Certification Course Guide module shown.*

**4. Lesson planning & structure**

Every lesson follows a consistent shape so swimmers know what to expect
and the water is managed safely: **entry & safety check → warm-up &
review → skill focus (short, high-repetition blocks) → application
(combine with established skills) → cool-down, recap & controlled
exit.** Each session has a clear objective drawn from the level's IRD
focus, and every activity has a purpose.

*Requirement:* an instructor plans each session on paper against the
level's IRD focus, with a purpose for every activity. *(Template: Annex
B4. Taught in Course Guide M4.)*

**5. Class management & supervision**

*Requirement:* an instructor maintains **constant visual supervision**
of every swimmer; keeps the **instructor-to-swimmer ratio appropriate to
level and ability** (Annex B3), lower at higher and deeper levels; keeps
everyone moving in small sets; never leaves the waterside while swimmers
are in the water; and uses clear, age-appropriate language and
demonstration. *(Taught in Course Guide M4; positioning in M5.)*

**6. Safe teaching methods**

*Requirement:* an instructor teaches from the position that keeps every
swimmer in continuous view — defaulting to the poolside and entering the
water only by risk-assessed decision — maintains an open, visible
posture with hands in sight, uses hands-on support only with consent and
by an approved method, presents professionally, and never teaches in the
water unobserved or beyond safe fatigue limits. *(Taught in Course Guide
M5; figure in Appendix G.)*

**7. Instructor safety responsibilities**

*Requirement:* an instructor foresees and controls risk before swimmers
enter the water; knows each venue's rules, emergency plan, and safety
equipment; supervises to a safe ratio with constant visual supervision;
knows each swimmer's developmental stage and declared conditions; uses
equipment correctly; and holds current lifesaving and First Aid/CPR
certification, ready to act on the Emergency Action Plan. *(Taught in
Course Guide M6; tools in Appendix E.)*

**8. Water safety & safeguarding in delivery**

• **Water safety** is integral: safe entry/exit, breath control,
floating, and self-rescue are taught from Level 1 and revisited at every
level, consistent with the survival-skill strand of the Syllabus.

• **Safeguarding** is a hard requirement of the BADGES Manual and binds
every instructor: parental consent before participation; a child is
never isolated one-to-one out of sight; a child's information is never
disclosed publicly; concerns are escalated immediately to the
**Designated Safeguarding Lead** (interim: the Chairperson, per SG-1).
Instructors uphold these in lessons exactly as examiners do on
assessment day.

**9. Good teaching practice**

*Requirement:* an instructor applies established good practice — builds
confidence and trust, keeps every swimmer motivated and moving, teaches
sound progressions with readiness assessed before advancing,
communicates clearly and constructively, plans each session with
purpose, and rewards effort as well as success. *(Taught in Course Guide
M8; quick-reference in Appendix F.)*

**10. Tracking progress & readiness**

• Instructors track each swimmer's progress through the IRD phases per
skill, so readiness is an evidenced judgement, not a guess.

• The instructor **validates readiness** before booking — confirming,
candidate by candidate, that every criterion at the target level is met
independently.

• Progress records support the conversation with families about the next
step and its timing.

**11. Inclusion & adapting for needs**

Instruction is adapted to a swimmer's needs **without lowering the
standard**: pace, support, and communication are adjusted; the
competency required at each level is not. Significant or permanent
conditions affecting participation are declared and, where they bear on
assessment, flagged in advance per the Assessment System Handbook §3.10.

**PART III — THE SYSTEM**

**12. The instructor's role in the Portal**

An instructor's administrative work runs through the BADGES Portal:

• **Register candidates** — a candidate is registered by their
instructor or centre (never directly by a parent); an unregistered
candidate cannot be assessed.

• **Issue the claim slip** — the Portal generates a claim slip for the
family to link to their child's record; the instructor hands it over.
The claim code is never printed on a certificate.

• **Confirm readiness and book the assessment** — the instructor
validates each candidate and submits the booking, at least 30 days
before the date, per the Assessment System Handbook.

• **Hold the separation of roles** — the instructor prepares and books;
the assigned examiner (never the candidate's own instructor) assesses.

**13. Preparing swimmers for assessment**

An instructor prepares a swimmer not only in skill but in the **shape of
the assessment**, so the day holds no surprises. Each level has an
ordered run-through of its criteria (the BADGES Assessment Sequences,
taught in Course Guide M11). The instructor rehearses the flow *after*
the skills are owned — never drilling the sequence to manufacture a
pass. The authoritative procedure and criteria live in the Assessment
System Handbook and the Syllabus.

**ANNEXES**

**Annex A — Per-level teaching focus (seeded from the Syllabus)**

*Restates the Syllabus's Introduce/Reinforce/Develop objectives as the
teaching focus per level; the seed for the drill library (B1).*

• **L1 Starfish.** *Introduce:* pool rules; safe entries/exits; breath
control; basic flotation; basic mobility; stationary survival.
*Develop:* water confidence; mobility; survival skill.

• **L2 Sea Turtle.** *Introduce:* self-survival to the wall; basic
front/back mobility. *Reinforce:* safe entry/exit; body position; front
flutter kick; lateral rotation. *Develop:* fall-in-turn-grab; basic
front crawl/backstroke arms; back flutter; reach-and-pull.

• **L3 Guppy.** *Introduce:* front crawl with breathing; underwater
mobility; deep-water survival. *Reinforce:* consistent front
crawl/backstroke; efficient survival. *Develop:* unilateral breathing;
efficient backstroke; underwater push-off; survival mobility.

• **L4 Octopus.** *Introduce:* bilateral breathing; breaststroke whip
kick; sit dive; intermediate survival. *Reinforce:* efficient front
crawl/backstroke; streamlined underwater. *Develop:* distance; sit-dive
progression; survival mobility.

• **L5 Frog.** *Introduce:* breaststroke; dolphin kick; sculling; flip
turn; squat dive. *Reinforce:* efficient strokes; dive; underwater.
*Develop:* distance; full breaststroke; efficient dive; survival
mobility.

• **L6 Swordfish.** *Introduce:* breaststroke pull-out; butterfly;
tumble/open turns; stand dive; sidestroke. *Reinforce:* efficient first
three strokes; flip turn; dive; sculling. *Develop:* distance; full
butterfly; turns out of the wall.

• **L7 Dolphin.** *Introduce:* high-elbow front crawl; competitive
backstroke pull; backstroke dive; IM; time qualification. *Reinforce:*
four strokes; turns; dives. *Develop:* four-stroke distance; IM
sequence.

**Annex B1 — Drill & progression library**

*Progressions in IRD order, mapped to the levels each skill lives in.
Pairs with the correction cues in B2.*

• **Water confidence & breath control (L1–L2).** *I:* surface bubbles →
hum-bubbles → submersion bob. *R:* rhythmic bobbing to a count; open
eyes underwater; retrieve a sinking toy. *D:* ten continuous bobs; hold
a calm shape 3 s.

• **Balance & body position (L1–L3).** *I:* star float front/back with
support; mushroom float. *R:* independent floats to a count; roll
front↔back. *D:* streamlined horizontal hold; controlled recovery to
standing.

• **Streamline & glide (L1–L5).** *I:* supported push to a 2 m target.
*R:* independent push-and-glide, arms locked, 3–5 m. *D:* underwater
push-off; add first kick into stroke.

• **Kicks (L1–L5).** *I:* flutter on the wall then board; supported back
flutter. *R:* flutter 5–10 m front/back from the hips; dolphin body-wave
on the wall. *D:* whip kick wall→back→board; dolphin 10 m; vertical kick
(treading lead-in).

• **Front crawl (L2–L7).** *I:* single-arm with board; catch-up; face-in
glide. *R:* unilateral breathing 10 m; long reach on train-tracks;
steady 6-beat kick. *D:* bilateral breathing; 15→25→50→100 m; high-elbow
catch; pacing for the L7 time.

• **Backstroke (L2–L7).** *I:* supported back flutter; single-arm;
still-head drill. *R:* straight-arm recovery 10–15 m; hips up. *D:*
25→50→100 m; competitive bent-arm pull; wall/block start.

• **Breaststroke (L4–L7).** *I:* whip kick on wall/back;
pull-breathe-kick-glide in parts. *R:* full stroke 25 m with a clear
glide; symmetrical kick. *D:* 50→100 m; underwater pull-out off each
wall.

• **Butterfly (L5–L7).** *I:* body-wave/dolphin kick; kick-hands-in/out;
single-arm. *R:* 3-kick-1-arm build; 10 m full stroke, two kicks per
pull. *D:* 25→50 m; hip-led timing; clean recovery.

• **Sculling & treading (L3–L7).** *I:* figure-8 sculls at the wall;
vertical scull with kick. *R:* tread 30 s; head-/feet-first sculling.
*D:* tread 2 min relaxed; scull + flutter + breathing 15 m.

• **Diving & entries (L4–L7).** *I:* sit dive to a target; kneel dive.
*R:* squat dive into streamline; add dolphin kick. *D:* stand dive into
stroke; backstroke start entry.

• **Turns (L5–L7).** *I:* somersault away from the wall; open-turn
walk-through. *R:* open turn in/out with streamline and kicks. *D:*
timed tumble off the flags; open turns for breast/fly.

• **Survival & water safety (all levels).** *I:* safe entries/exits;
float-signal-call. *R:* survival backstroke; reach-and-pull; treading
with clothing awareness. *D:* longer survival swims; combined sequences;
non-contact reach rescue.

**Annex B2 — Common faults & correction cues**

*Fault → likely cause → child-facing cue. Correct the cause, not the
symptom. Cues support teaching; the Syllabus owns what is assessed.*

• **Front/back float — hips sink:** head lifted / empty lungs / tension
→ "Look down (or up), belly to the sky, big lungs; ears in on your
back."

• **Streamline — arms apart, quick sink:** no lock → "Squeeze your ears,
hide your chin, point like an arrow."

• **Flutter kick — big knee bend, no travel:** kicking from the knee,
stiff ankles → "Long legs, floppy feet, kick from the hips — small and
fast."

• **Front crawl — hand crosses the centre-line:** reaching across →
"Reach past your shoulder — swim on train-tracks."

• **Front crawl — dropped elbow, short pull:** no catch → "High elbow,
fingertips down, pull past your hip." (catch-up / single-arm)

• **Front crawl — lifts head to breathe:** head up not rotating → "Turn,
don't lift — keep one goggle in."

• **Backstroke — sits / pikes:** head & hips low → "Head still, ears in,
hips up, tummy to the sky."

• **Backstroke — bent-arm entry:** no straight recovery → "Thumb out,
little-finger in, brush your ear."

• **Breaststroke — screw kick:** asymmetric turn-out → "Heels to your
bottom, feet turned out, whip and squeeze evenly."

• **Breaststroke — no glide:** timing collapsed → "Pull — breathe — kick
— **glide**; be a torpedo between strokes."

• **Butterfly — arms-only, stalls:** no hip drive → "Hips lead — two
kicks, one pull; kick your hands in and out."

• **Sculling — stiff wrists:** no feel → "Paint figure-8s, tilt your
palms — feel the water push back."

• **Dive — belly flop:** chin up, no aim → "Hide your chin, squeeze your
ears, aim your fingers at a spot."

• **Tumble turn — mistimed:** judging late → "Pick your marker, last
stroke in, tuck and flip; feet plant, arrow, push."

**Annex B3 — Teaching ratios & group sizes**

**Maximum 1:6 (industry standard, Sign-off SO-01).** Rules that always
apply:

• **In-water teaching is capped at 1:6** — never exceeded.

• A ratio is **reduced, never raised**, by risk assessment: shallow
experience, deep water, nervous or younger swimmers, mixed ability, or a
challenging venue all lower it.

• A **competent responder is always on the poolside** whenever swimmers
are in the water.

• **Poolside teaching of independent swimmers** may run a little higher
only where continuous supervision is genuinely maintained; the 1:6
standard is the reference maximum.

| **Level(s)**               | **Typical water**    | **Recommended max** | **Notes**                                             |
|----------------------------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| 1–2 (Starfish, Sea Turtle) | Shallow              | 1:4–1:6             | Beginners; go to 1:4 for very young/nervous.          |
| 3 (Guppy)                  | Shallow + deep intro | 1:6                 | Reduce in deep water.                                 |
| 4–5 (Octopus, Frog)        | Shallow / deep       | 1:6                 | Deep-water work keeps a dedicated observer.           |
| 6–7 (Swordfish, Dolphin)   | Deep                 | 1:6                 | More independent; deep-water safety cover maintained. |

*These teaching ratios are separate from — and tighter than — the
per-examiner assessment caps on the day (Assessment System Handbook
Annex B), which govern how many candidates one examiner assesses, not
teaches.*

**Annex B4 — Session-plan template & scheme-of-work**

**One-lesson template.** Level/group · date · duration · ratio · session
objective (from the level's IRD focus) · prior-session recap. Then the
five phases — entry & safety check (2–3 min) · warm-up & review (5 min,
Reinforce) · skill focus (10–12 min, Introduce/Reinforce) · application
(8–10 min, Develop) · cool-down, recap & exit (3–5 min) — each with its
skill focus and a safety/positioning note. Close with notes on
individual readiness.

**Scheme-of-work (a level across a term).** Weeks 1–2 **Introduce** the
level's new skills and assess entry readiness → Weeks 3–5 **Reinforce**
to consistency, weaving the water-safety strand → Weeks 6–8 **Develop**
to the Syllabus distance/independence and combine skills → ongoing,
**validate readiness** before booking.

**Annex C — Instructor competency checklist**

*A one-page confirmation of the Part II requirements. A certified
instructor can:*

• ☐ Plan and run a safe, progressive lesson to the standard shape, with
a purpose for every activity.

• ☐ Maintain continuous visual supervision at a ratio appropriate to
level and ability (max 1:6).

• ☐ Teach every level through the IRD method, building technique, not
teaching to the test.

• ☐ Choose a safe, visible teaching position and keep every swimmer in
line of sight.

• ☐ Use hands-on support only with consent and by an approved hold.

• ☐ Foresee and control risk; know the venue's emergency plan; act on
it; hold current lifesaving/First Aid-CPR.

• ☐ Uphold water safety and safeguarding, escalating concerns to the
DSL.

• ☐ Apply good teaching practice — confidence, motivation, clear
communication, reward.

• ☐ Track progress through the IRD phases and validate readiness before
booking.

• ☐ Adapt for individual needs without lowering the standard.

• ☐ Operate the Portal — register candidates, issue claim slips, book
assessments — never assessing their own swimmers (the separation of
roles).

**On the BADGES Platform Portal**

The Portal is where the instructor's preparation becomes a system
record: candidate registration, claim-slip issuance, readiness
confirmation, and assessment booking. It enforces the registration gate
(no unregistered candidate is assessable) and the separation of roles
(the booking instructor is never the assessing examiner). This Handbook
describes the teaching practice; the Portal carries the administration.
