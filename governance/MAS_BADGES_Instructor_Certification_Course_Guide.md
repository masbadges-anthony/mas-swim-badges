# BADGES Instructor Certification Course Guide

**Edition 2.0 · 28 June 2026**
Supersedes Edition 1.0 (*BADGES Instructor Foundation Certification Course Guide*).
Delivered by the **BADGES System Master Trainer**. Trains to the **Teaching System
Handbook** (doctrine) and the **7-Level Syllabus** (standard); feeds the **Examiner
Certification Course Guide** (prerequisite). Executed in the **BADGES Platform Portal**
(instructor onboarding loop).

> **Edition 2.0 incorporates the Governance Decisions Register v1.0 (28 Jun 2026):**
> OD-1 (this certification is the single instructor credential; the word "Foundation"
> is dropped), OD-1a (founding examiner prerequisite), and OD-2 (single unified Master
> Trainer — **Mr. Melvin Chua**, holding both the instructor and examiner streams).

---

## 1. Outcomes — what a certified instructor can do

On certification, a BADGES Instructor can:

- Teach all seven levels using the Introduce → Reinforce → Develop method, building
  genuine, transferable technique to the Syllabus standard.
- Plan and run safe, well-managed lessons with appropriate supervision and ratios.
- Uphold water safety and safeguarding throughout delivery.
- Judge readiness honestly and prepare a swimmer for independent assessment without
  teaching to the test.
- Operate the BADGES Portal: register candidates, issue claim slips, confirm readiness,
  and book assessments — while staying firewalled from judging their own swimmers.

---

## 2. Eligibility and prerequisites

The **BADGES Instructor certification** is awarded through this course; it is the
single, current instructor credential and the national standard (OD-1). A candidate
should:

- Meet the **swimming-competency baseline** for an instructor — able to demonstrate the
  four strokes and the survival/skill elements across the levels to a competent
  standard. *(Exact demonstration standard to be confirmed by the Coaching Panel.)*
- Hold a valid **First Aid and CPR**, **Bronze Medallion**, or equivalent lifesaving
  certification — the same lifesaving baseline an instructor must maintain to act as a
  Water-Safety Supervisor on assessment day.
- Be in **Good Standing** — no current MAS disciplinary action and no disqualifying
  criminal record; signs a Good Standing declaration and completes child-protection
  screening.

> **Legacy instructors.** A prior MAS instructor certification is **recognised for
> standing**, but does not by itself confer the BADGES Instructor certification, because
> the syllabus content is materially new. Legacy instructors gain the BADGES Instructor
> certification — and the public directory listing — by sitting this course as a
> **refresher at a discounted course fee** (OD-1).

> **Founding phase.** During inaugural rollout, MAS recruits from instructors who
> completed earlier MAS Instructor Courses and from active swim-school teaching staff,
> admitting them through the founding-cohort refresher route consistent with the pilot
> recruitment plan.

---

## 3. Application pathway

1. Submit the **Instructor Application** in the Portal with proof of the
   swimming-competency baseline, lifesaving certification, the Good Standing
   declaration, and child-protection screening.
2. Pay the course registration fee (legacy instructors: the **discounted refresher
   rate**). The quantum is held in the Portal fee schedule.
3. Attend the full **BADGES Instructor Certification Course** (§5).
4. Meet the **pass requirements** (§6).
5. On certification, the Master Trainer marks the candidate certified, which triggers
   Portal onboarding (§8) and eligibility for the public instructor directory (§7).

---

## 4. The Master Trainer

The **Master Trainer** develops and delivers the BADGES certification courses, operating
in cycles tied to intakes. The role is unified (OD-2): a single Master Trainer trains
**both instructors and examiners** — this Guide is the instructor stream. Inaugural
Master Trainer: **Mr. Melvin Chua**. Responsibilities for the instructor stream:

- Develop course methodology and materials with the Coaching Panel's approval.
- Plan and deliver each intake, classroom and poolside.
- Lead the practical teaching assessment and recommend certification.
- Submit a course report to the Coaching Panel after each intake.
- Refresh materials in line with Teaching System Handbook and Syllabus changes.

> Where more than one Master Trainer is appointed, one holder (or the Coaching Panel) is
> curriculum custodian, and separate intakes calibrate against each other so all
> trainers certify to one bar (OD-2).

## 5. The course — format and modules

**Format.** A multi-day course combining classroom and poolside delivery, with class
size capped to allow individual practical teaching assessment of every participant.
Attendance at every module is **compulsory**; a missed module means repeating the full
course. No partial completions.

| Mod. | Title | Mode | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Program & Governance | Classroom | MAS structure and the BADGES document system; the 7-level pathway; where the instructor fits; the instructor/examiner firewall. |
| 2 | The 7-Level Syllabus, taught | Classroom + Pool | Walk-through of every level's outcomes from a *teaching* lens; what "ready" looks like per level. |
| 3 | The IRD Teaching Method | Classroom + Pool | Introduce → Reinforce → Develop; building technique, not teaching to the test; common faults and correction cues. |
| 4 | Lesson Delivery & Class Management | Classroom + Pool | Lesson structure; supervision; ratios; language and demonstration; managing mixed-ability groups safely. |
| 5 | Water Safety & Safeguarding | Classroom | Water safety integral to every level; child protection and safeguarding obligations; consent; escalation. |
| 6 | Conduct & Professionalism | Classroom | Honest preparation and readiness; respecting examiner independence; transparency with families; use of MAS marks. |
| 7 | **BADGES Portal for Instructors** | Hands-on | Registering candidates; issuing the claim slip; confirming readiness; booking an assessment; what the instructor can and cannot see; the firewall in practice. |
| 8 | Practical Teaching Assessment | Pool | Each candidate delivers a real teaching segment under observation by the Master Trainer. Direct feedback; final certification recommendation. |

---

## 6. Assessment and pass requirements

To be certified as a BADGES Instructor, a candidate must:

- **Attend every module in full.**
- **Pass the theory assessment** covering the Syllabus (taught lens), the IRD method,
  safety, and conduct. *(Pass mark to be confirmed by the Coaching Panel; recommend
  aligning with the examiner course's 80%.)*
- **Demonstrate Portal competency** in Module 7 — register a candidate, issue a claim
  slip, and create a booking correctly.
- **Achieve Competent or above** on the practical teaching assessment in Module 8,
  judged by the Master Trainer.
- **Sign the conduct undertaking** and complete child-protection screening.

---

## 7. Certification, directory listing, term, and continuing certification

- **Certification** is recorded by the Master Trainer on completion and triggers Portal
  onboarding (§8).
- **Public directory listing.** A certified BADGES Instructor may **opt in** to the
  public instructor directory. A legacy MAS instructor certification alone is recognised
  for standing but is **not directory-listed** until the holder gains the BADGES
  Instructor certification through this course (OD-1).
- **Continuing certification** requires maintaining current lifesaving and First
  Aid/CPR certification, upholding safeguarding standards, and completing any refresher
  modules issued after Teaching System Handbook or Syllabus updates. *(Renewal cycle to
  be confirmed — recommend aligning with the MAS two-year governance term.)*
- A BADGES Instructor certification is the prerequisite the **Examiner Certification
  Course Guide** builds on. **Founding-cohort exception (OD-1a):** a recognised legacy
  instructor certification is accepted to enter the examiner course, and completing that
  course confers BADGES Instructor standing — so the examiner is directory-listed without
  a separate instructor refresher.

---

## 8. Onboarding into the Portal

Certification is the front door to the system, implemented as the instructor-invitation
loop:

1. On certification, the candidate's record moves to **certified** in the Portal.
2. An **invitation** keyed to the instructor's email is issued.
3. The instructor **self-signs-up**; the signup is matched to the invitation, and the
   **instructor role is granted**.
4. The instructor can now register candidates, issue claim slips, and book assessments.

The course and this onboarding are two halves of one pathway: the Course Guide certifies
the person; the Portal admits them.

---

## 9. On the BADGES Platform Portal

Instructor competency on the Portal is a certified outcome (Module 7), because the
instructor's administrative role is entirely Portal-based: candidate registration (the
assessable-eligibility gate), claim-slip issuance to families, readiness confirmation,
and assessment booking. The Portal enforces the firewall — a booking instructor is never
assigned to assess their own candidates. This Guide certifies the capability; the Portal
is where it is exercised.
